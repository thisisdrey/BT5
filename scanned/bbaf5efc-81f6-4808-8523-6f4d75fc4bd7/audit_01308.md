# Malformed `op::get_fees_notification` Payload in `fee_manager` Breaks Fee Updates

## Summary
This report describes a bug in the `fee_manager` contract that can cause issues with fee updates and contract operations. The bug is caused by the `fee_manager` contract deviating from the expected format used by the `factory` contract. This deviation can lead to failed fee updates or bounced messages, potentially causing denial of service. The recommendation is to reconstruct the `op::get_fees_notification` payload to match the expected format and remove any additional fields.

## Details
**Update**
The team added the `query_id` to the `op::get_fees_notification` payload. The client mentioned that some contracts may need to retrieve the `admin_address` or a custom payload. Although these are optional, they prefer to keep the data in the payload.

Addressed in: `b101b967bc6025143cc19fa3f177aca5edb65e1f`.

**File(s) affected:**`contracts/fee_manager.fc`

**Description:** The `recv_internal()` handler for `op::get_fees` in `fee_manager` constructs the `op::get_fees_notification` payload with extra fields (`.store_slice(data::admin_address)` and `.store_slice(in_msg_body)`) right after the fee dictionary, deviating from the expected format used by `factory`, which expects a `query_id`. As a result, the `factory` contract cannot parse the response correctly, leading to failed fee updates or bounced messages, which can disable contract operations that depend on up-to-date fee data.

**Exploit Scenario:**

1.   `factory` sends `op::get_fees` to `fee_manager`.
2.   `fee_manager` responds with a malformed payload.
3.   `factory` fails to parse `data::current_fees` or bounces the message, leading to stale fee settings and potential denial of service.

**Recommendation:** Reconstruct the `op::get_fees_notification` payload to match the expected shape:

```
cell payload = begin_cell()
  .store_op(op::get_fees_notification)
  .store_uint(query_id, 64)
  .store_dict(data::fees)
.end_cell();
```

Remove any additional fields and ensure the notification aligns with `factory`’s parsing logic.
