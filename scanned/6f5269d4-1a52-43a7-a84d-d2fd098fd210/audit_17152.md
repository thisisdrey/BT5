# Front Running Between Purchase & Reclaim Bribe . . . . . .

## Summary
The report describes a bug in the Vault contract where an attacker can manipulate the bribe system to incentivize traders to buy tickets without actually losing the bribe amount. This is done by offering a bribe for a specific epoch or ticket and then quickly reclaiming it before the purchase transaction is completed. This results in the trader not receiving their expected bribe payout. To fix this issue, the reclaim bribe functions have been modified to only reclaim for past epochs. This fix has been implemented in the latest version of the contract.

## Details
## Vault Contract Bribe Mechanism

In the Vault contract, bribes are used to incentivize a purchase for a specific epoch or ticket. There are two types of bribes available:

1. **offerGeneralBribe**: A bribe for an epoch.
2. **offerConcentratedBribe**: A bribe for a specific ticketId in an epoch.

The `reclaimGeneralBribe` and `reclaimConcentratedBribe` functions are used to reclaim the bribe if a purchase does not occur for that epoch or ticketId. An attacker can abuse this by offering a bribe, then front-running the purchase transaction request by quickly reclaiming the bribe.

## Proof of Concept

To reproduce the issue, follow the steps below:

1. Attacker offers a bribe with `reclaimGeneralBribe` for epoch A.
2. Trader purchases tickets on epoch A (expecting to obtain the bribe).
3. Attacker front-runs the purchase transaction with `reclaimGeneralBribe`.
4. The trader’s purchase happens after `reclaimGeneralBribe`, so the trader does not get their `bribePayout`.

With this bug, the attacker manages to incentivize traders to buy tickets for an epoch without actually losing the bribe amount.

## Remediation

Modify the `reclaimGeneralBribe` and `reclaimConcentratedBribe` functions to reclaim only for past epochs.

## Patch

Modified reclaim bribe functions to reclaim only for past epochs. Fixed in `9d73ec2`.
