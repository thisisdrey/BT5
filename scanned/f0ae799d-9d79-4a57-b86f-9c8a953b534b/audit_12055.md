# Vouchers Can Be Frontrun

## Summary
The client has reported a bug where vouchers can be used to mint NFTs to any address, regardless of who the voucher was originally intended for. This can lead to malicious actors using the voucher before the intended user, causing their transaction to fail. The bug affects the `NFT721`, `NFT1155`, and `NFTMarketSimple` files. The recommendation is to confirm if this is the intended design and consider adding the address where the NFT will be minted to the voucher or adding an owner who must be the voucher's signer to make them valid.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Acknowledged" by the client. The client provided the following explanation:

> Vouchers Can Be Frontrun - This is exactly the logic we need for our NFT marketplace, as we do not know who will buy a particular token at the time of voucher/plain listing creation.

**File(s) affected:**`NFT721`, `NFT1155`, `NFTMarketSimple`

**Description:** Redeeming vouchers only requires that the signature of the voucher matches that of the creator of the voucher. The address the NFT is sent to is completely independent of the voucher. Therefore, any voucher can be used to mint an NFT to any address. Since all of the voucher's data will be available to the mempool when calling `redeem()`, a malicious actor could use the voucher before the user who created and sent the transaction with the voucher. In the case of `NFT721`, the original user's transaction will fail due to the ID specified already having been used. `NFT1155` transactions may also fail if the frontrunner mints the full amount in the voucher. Vouchers for `NFT1155` may also be reused if the first transaction did not claim the full amount.

**Recommendation:** Confirm that this is the intended design. If not, consider making the address to which the NFT(s) will be minted part of the voucher. Consider adding an owner who must be the voucher's signer to make them valid.
