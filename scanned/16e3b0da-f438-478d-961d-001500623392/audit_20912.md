# [M06] Underlying tokens could get stuck

## Summary
The Incentivizer contract allows users to receive reward tokens when staking underlying assets into the contract. However, due to human nature, users may transfer directly those underlying tokens with a simple ERC20 transfer transaction instead of calling the stake function. This means that the underlying assets sent through a regular transfer transaction will get stuck in the contract without any possibility of taking it back. To prevent this, a pull request was made to implement the possibility to withdraw those stuck assets from the contract. This was then fixed with pull request 14.

## Details
The [`Incentivizer` contract](https://github.com/emptysetsquad/emptyset/blob/bf9753ef9cd5b17236036257f290e0d0b850a029/protocol/contracts/src/incentivizer/Incentivizer.sol#L35) allows users to receive reward tokens when [staking underlying assets](https://github.com/emptysetsquad/emptyset/blob/bf9753ef9cd5b17236036257f290e0d0b850a029/protocol/contracts/src/incentivizer/Incentivizer.sol#L284) into the contract.


However, because the contract [requests underlying assets to be transferred](https://github.com/emptysetsquad/emptyset/blob/bf9753ef9cd5b17236036257f290e0d0b850a029/protocol/contracts/src/incentivizer/Incentivizer.sol#L292) into the contract in order to proceed, and due to human nature, users may transfer directly those underlying tokens with a simple ERC20 transfer transaction instead of calling the `stake` function.


Although the contract has a function that can be used to move any token into the reserve, the [`rescue` function](https://github.com/emptysetsquad/emptyset/blob/bf9753ef9cd5b17236036257f290e0d0b850a029/protocol/contracts/src/incentivizer/Incentivizer.sol#L176), this function works with any token [except the underlying asset](https://github.com/emptysetsquad/emptyset/blob/bf9753ef9cd5b17236036257f290e0d0b850a029/protocol/contracts/src/incentivizer/Incentivizer.sol#L177). Therefore, any underlying asset sent through a regular transfer transaction will get stuck in the contract without any possibility of taking it back.


Because underlying assets may be sent directly into the contract on a daily basis , consider implementing the possibility to withdraw those stuck assets from the contract.


***Update**: Fixed on [pull request 14](https://github.com/emptysetsquad/emptyset/pull/14/commits/228a81bbee212cabf4ea02a3f475650cd296f455).*
