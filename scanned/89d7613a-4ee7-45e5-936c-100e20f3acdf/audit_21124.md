# [H01] Users participating in Genesis can get fewer FEI and TRIBE than expected

## Summary
This bug report is about the `GenesisGroup` contract, which is used to bootstrap the Fei Protocol. The bug is that the contract continues to accept `purchase` calls after the maximum price of `FEI` has been reached. This could cause contributors to receive fewer `FEI` and `TRIBE` tokens than expected, and even `FEI` valued at less than their ETH investment. To fix the issue, a check was added to the `purchase` function to prevent it from being executed if the purchase causes the `maxGenesisPrice` to be exceeded. Additionally, the initial `FEI` price was capped in the `BondingCurve` contract so that it does not exceed the buffer adjusted amount. This bug was fixed in PR#24.

## Details
The [`GenesisGroup` contract](https://github.com/fei-protocol/fei-protocol-core/blob/29aeefddd97f31c7f2a598fb3dca3ef24dc0beb4/contracts/genesis/GenesisGroup.sol) stops the genesis period earlier than its `duration`, if enough ETH has been contributed to reach the [*maximum price of `FEI` paid by the Genesis Group*](https://github.com/fei-protocol/fei-protocol-core/blob/29aeefddd97f31c7f2a598fb3dca3ef24dc0beb4/contracts/genesis/GenesisGroup.sol#L46). This attractive maximum price incentivizes users to contribute to the `GenesisGroup` to bootstrap the protocol.


However, the `GenesisGroup` continues to accept [`purchase`](https://github.com/fei-protocol/fei-protocol-core/blob/29aeefddd97f31c7f2a598fb3dca3ef24dc0beb4/contracts/genesis/GenesisGroup.sol#L80) calls after the [`maxGenesisPrice`](https://github.com/fei-protocol/fei-protocol-core/blob/29aeefddd97f31c7f2a598fb3dca3ef24dc0beb4/contracts/genesis/GenesisGroup.sol#L37) has been reached. Even with a solution to **[ETH can get stuck in the `GenesisGroup` contract issue](#ethstuckingenesis)**, `purchase` transactions will continue to be accepted until the [`launch`](https://github.com/fei-protocol/fei-protocol-core/blob/29aeefddd97f31c7f2a598fb3dca3ef24dc0beb4/contracts/genesis/GenesisGroup.sol#L105) function is called. These additional purchases could push the average price for the `GenesisGroup` far above the `maxGenesisPrice`, causing contributors to receive fewer `FEI` and `TRIBE` tokens than expected. In extreme cases, users could end up receiving `FEI` that is valued at less than their ETH investment (measured in USD).


Consider implementing a check in the `purchase` function that prevents it from being executed if the purchase causes the `maxGenesisPrice` to be exceeded. Notably, if the maximum price has not been reached, but a purchase would cause the maximum price to be exceeded, some or all of the purchase amount should be rejected.


**Update:** *Fixed in [PR#24](https://github.com/fei-protocol/fei-protocol-core/pull/24). The Fei Team decided to remove the `isAtMaxPrice` condition to allow the protocol to be launched before the genesis period passed, and added a condition to check whether the genesis was launched when calling the `purchase` function. Additionally, the initial `FEI` price has been capped in the `BondingCurve` contract so that [it does not exceed the buffer adjusted amount](https://github.com/fei-protocol/fei-protocol-core/pull/24/files#diff-9209a43c4f6e3fb7c3d78f0908f50ddae86c09e8da2a108d5ed4e548879f2885R93), which means that if enough ETH is collected in genesis, users will not benefit from a discount in `FEI`.*
