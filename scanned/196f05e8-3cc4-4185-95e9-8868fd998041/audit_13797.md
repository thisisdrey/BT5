# Basis Point Fee Misleading

## Summary
The Fabric team recently fixed an issue where the yield fee was not being calculated correctly. The formula used to calculate the fee was not giving the correct percentage to the fee recipient, resulting in them receiving less than the intended amount. This could potentially lead to exploitation, where the fee recipient would receive less than what they were supposed to. The team has implemented a new formula to fix this issue and ensure that the fee recipient receives the correct percentage of the yield fee.

## Details
**Update**
The Fabric team fixed the issue in commit `f1d9552` by implementing our recommendation and calculating the `feeAllocation` using the following formula: 
```
_contributionTotal * _feeYieldBips / (MAX_BIPS - _feeYieldBips)
```

**Description:** The way the yield fee is calculated is by increasing the total contribution by adding a percentage of the total contributions and awarding it to the fee recipient as follows:

```
uint256 feeAllocation = (_contributionTotal * _feeYieldBips) / (MAX_BIPS);

_contributions[_feeRecipient] += feeAllocation;
_contributionTotal += feeAllocation;
```

However, this formula means that the fee recipient is taking a value less than `_feeYield / MAX_BIPS`.

**Exploit Scenario:** Assume a user contributed `1000` tokens and `_feeYieldBips == 1000` (i.e. 10%). Since 10% of `1000` is `100` the total contributed would now be `1100` after the `allocateYieldFee()`. Since the fee recipient contributed `100` and the total is `1100` the fee recipient will receive 100/1100 = 9.09% of the total yield and not 10%.

**Recommendation:** The formula for working out exactly how much contribution the `_feeRecipient` should be awarded to that they collect the correct percentage is given as follows:

```
x/x + total_contributions = yield_fee_percentage
```

Solving for x gives us that the amount contributed should be given by:

```
total_contribution * yield_fee_percentage / (1 - yield_fee_percentage)
```
