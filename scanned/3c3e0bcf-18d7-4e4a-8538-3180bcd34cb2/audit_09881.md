# Inconsistency In Credit Deduction

## Summary
The vulnerability in this system concerns a flaw in the credit calculation mechanism during subscription upgrades, which can result in a loss of provider earnings. When users upgrade their subscription plan, the function "upgradeSubscriptionPlan" is called. However, a flaw exists in this process where the latest subscription considered may not be the one before the upgrade, but instead the upgraded subscription. This can lead to incorrect credit calculations and potentially result in providers receiving lower earnings than they deserve. The issue has been fixed in a recent patch.

## Details
## Vulnerability Concern in Credit Calculation

The vulnerability concerns a flaw in the credit calculation mechanism during subscription upgrades, resulting in a potential loss of provider earnings. When users upgrade their subscription plan, `upgradeSubscriptionPlan` is called. During the upgrade process, the Consumer-Unit (CU) tracker timer for the previous subscription is set using `addCuTrackerTimerForSubscription`. Subsequently, after the subscription upgrade, `RewardAndResetCuTracker` is activated at a later block `(nextEpoch+blocksToSave-1)`, updating the credit for the latest subscription.

```go
func (k Keeper) upgradeSubscriptionPlan(ctx sdk.Context, sub *types.Subscription, newPlan *planstypes.Plan) error {
    [...]
    // Track CU for the previous subscription
    k.addCuTrackerTimerForSubscription(ctx, block, sub)
    [...]
}
```

However, a flaw exists in this process where the latest subscription considered might not be the one before the upgrade; instead, it may be the upgraded subscription. Since the credit adjustment relies on the latest subscription, which may be the upgraded one, the credit is erroneously reduced. This miscalculation results in inaccuracies in credit processing, potentially resulting in providers to receive lower earnings than they deserve.

## Proof of Concept

1. There are two plans, Plan A (costing 1000 ulava), and Plan B (costing 1200 ulava).
2. On upgrading Plan A to Plan B via `upgradeSubscriptionPlan`, credits should be 1200 ulava.
3. Due to the flaw, the latest subscription considered is not the one before the upgrade (Plan A). Instead, it is the upgraded subscription (Plan B).
4. Thus, the initial credit matching the cost of Plan B (1200 ulava) is incorrectly reduced by the amount earned under Plan A (1000 ulava).
5. Consequently, the credit balance, instead of remaining at 1200 ulava to match the cost of Plan B, is reduced to 200 ulava after deducting the earnings from Plan A.

## Remediation

Avoid applying credit deductions to subscriptions after an upgrade.

## Patch

Fixed in b667 add.
