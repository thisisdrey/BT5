# `AccountManage` Can Be Initialized With No Required Signers

## Summary
A bug has been found in the `AccountManage` contract where the `signNum` is not being assigned a value during initialization. This means that anyone can withdraw NMT tokens without needing any signatures, which could result in a loss of tokens. The issue has been fixed in a recent update, but if the contract has already been deployed, the `signNum` must be manually updated using the `updateSignNum()` function. It is recommended to assign a non-zero value to `signNum` during initialization to prevent this issue from occurring.

## Details
**Update**
The client fixed the issue in commit `56fe445287d8dbde412d6d47fbfddfe070dabe6d` by initializing `signNum`to `2`. Since the `AccountManage` contract is already deployed, the client called `updateSignNum()` to set `signNum` to `2`.

**File(s) affected:**`AccountManage.sol`

**Description:** Currently, `AccountManage.init()` does not assign a `signNum` for the contract, meaning that after initialization, the `signNum` has a zero value. This means that `withdrawComputingFee()` can be invoked without any signatures necessary, allowing any address to withdraw the accumulated `providerFeeSum` amount of NMT tokens from this contract. During the audit, we notified the team of their live contract with a `signNum` of zero with ~18 NMT at stake for withdrawal by any party. As of 12 pm ET on July 12th, the `signNum` has not been updated and remains at a zero value.

**Recommendation:** During initialization of `AccountManage`, assign `signNum` a non-zero value. Note that if `AccountManage` is already deployed, `signNum` must be updated via `AccountManage.updateSignNum()`.
