# Reward Amount in GaugeExtraRewarder Can Be Overestimated

## Summary
The `GaugeExtraRewarder` contract has a function called `setDistributionRate` which checks if the contract has enough funds to cover the reward amount. However, it does not take into account any remaining reward amount from the previous distribution period, which can lead to the final reward amount being more than the contract's reserves. This bug has been fixed in a recent update.

## Details
The function [`setDistributionRate`](https://github.com/ThenafiBNB/THENA-Contracts/blob/2bb84cf6bb429e9bac8ac8464f4546da855ef4ca/contracts/GaugeExtraRewarder.sol#L119) of the `GaugeExtraRewarder` contract [first checks that the contract's balance is sufficient to cover the notified reward amount](https://github.com/ThenafiBNB/THENA-Contracts/blob/main/contracts/GaugeExtraRewarder.sol#L121). However, any remaining reward amount from the latest distribution period is not considered during this check, [as it is added to the total distributed reward amount only after this check](https://github.com/ThenafiBNB/THENA-Contracts/blob/main/contracts/GaugeExtraRewarder.sol#L128). As a consequence, the final reward amount to be distributed may exceed the contract's reserves.


Consider ensuring that the total reward amount can be covered by the contract's reserves.
