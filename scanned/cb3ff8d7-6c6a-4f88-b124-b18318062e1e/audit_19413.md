# [M04] Mishandled zero timestamp

## Summary
A bug has been identified in the LongShortPair contract, which is a part of the UMA Protocol. A zero early expiration timestamp is used as a flag to indicate that nobody has triggered the early expiration mechanism. However, it is possible to trigger the mechanism with a zero timestamp. This could lead to inconsistent settlements, and also emit a misleading event.

To fix this issue, a new commit was made to the UMA Protocol, [`11d287c07c93c04f534b2ef3c869966d9f18ac60`](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/pull/3526/commits/11d287c07c93c04f534b2ef3c869966d9f18ac60) in [PR3526](https://github.com/umaprotocol/protocol/pull/3526). This commit prevents early expiration using the zero timestamp.

## Details
In the `LongShortPair` contract, a zero early expiration timestamp is [used as a flag](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/d4e7ea22159b2eed8e39d5b86ce0026ea3b8b995/packages/core/contracts/financial-templates/long-short-pair/LongShortPair.sol#L349) to indicate that nobody has triggered the early expiration mechanism. However, it is possible to [trigger that mechanism](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/d4e7ea22159b2eed8e39d5b86ce0026ea3b8b995/packages/core/contracts/financial-templates/long-short-pair/LongShortPair.sol#L280) with a zero timestamp. In this scenario, the Optimistic Oracle will be be invoked but the [protection against subsequent price requests](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/d4e7ea22159b2eed8e39d5b86ce0026ea3b8b995/packages/core/contracts/financial-templates/long-short-pair/LongShortPair.sol#L110) will not be effective. Fortunately, once [the settlement price is chosen](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/d4e7ea22159b2eed8e39d5b86ce0026ea3b8b995/packages/core/contracts/financial-templates/long-short-pair/LongShortPair.sol#L241), it won’t be overriden, so this won’t lead to inconsistent settlements. Nevertheless, a subsequent price request could [change the recorded early expiration timestamp](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/d4e7ea22159b2eed8e39d5b86ce0026ea3b8b995/packages/core/contracts/financial-templates/long-short-pair/LongShortPair.sol#L289), even if the zero timestamp is used to determine the settlement price. It could also [emit a misleading event](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/d4e7ea22159b2eed8e39d5b86ce0026ea3b8b995/packages/core/contracts/financial-templates/long-short-pair/LongShortPair.sol#L304).


Consider preventing early expiration using the zero timestamp.


**Update:** *Fixed as of commit [`11d287c07c93c04f534b2ef3c869966d9f18ac60`](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/pull/3526/commits/11d287c07c93c04f534b2ef3c869966d9f18ac60) in [PR3526](https://github.com/umaprotocol/protocol/pull/3526).*
