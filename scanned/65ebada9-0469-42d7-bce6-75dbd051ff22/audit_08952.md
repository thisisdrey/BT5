# High Gas Consumption Risk in `Pool.totalAssets()` Function

## Summary
The report highlights a potential issue with the `Pool.totalAssets()` and `Pool.onGoingLoans()` functions in the `Pool.sol` file. These functions use looping logic that could lead to high gas consumption, potentially causing a Denial of Service (DoS) situation. To avoid this, it is recommended to find alternative strategies for calculating total assets and eliminate the looping logic. This will ensure efficient gas usage and prevent potential DoS scenarios.

## Details
**Update**
Acknowledged. Reply from the Nemeos team:

> _Extremely unlikely considering a `SLOAD`cost 5k and max block limit is over 30M. Gas optimizations are not a priority as we will be on Polygon._

We still recommend a more gas-efficient approach for the next iterations, or applying limits to the ongoing loans/liquidations.

**File(s) affected:**`Pool.sol`

**Description:** The `Pool.totalAssets()` function employs a looping logic over the `_ongoingLiquidations` and `_ongoingLoans` arrays to calculate the total assets, posing a significant risk of high gas consumption. This design could lead to reaching the block gas limit, effectively causing a Denial of Service (DoS) condition for critical functions of the main pool. In smart contract development, especially on platforms like Ethereum where gas costs are a crucial consideration, it's essential to optimize for efficiency and minimize operations that require extensive computational resources.

A similar issue applies to `Pool.onGoingLoans()`.

**Recommendation:** To prevent potential DoS scenarios and ensure efficient gas usage, it is recommended to eliminate looping logic in the `Pool.totalAssets()` function and seek alternative strategies for calculating total assets.
