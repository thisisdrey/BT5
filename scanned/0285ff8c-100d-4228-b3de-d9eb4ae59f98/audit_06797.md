# [M-01] The pending fee should be settled before adjusting the fee

## Summary
This bug report discusses a problem with functions related to fees in a program. These functions, such as `setFeeRecipient` and `setBaseFee`, can impact the distribution target and amount of the fee. If these changes are made without settling the pending fee first, it can cause errors in the fee settlement process. For example, if the current fee recipient is Alice and the pending fee is 100, changing the recipient to Bob will allocate the fee to Bob instead of Alice. To avoid this issue, it is recommended to settle the pending fee before making any changes to the fee.

## Details
**Severity**

**Impact:** Medium

**Likelihood:** Medium

**Description**

Functions related to fees, such as `setFeeRecipient`, `setBaseFee`, `setBaseFeeSplit`, and `setAffiliate`, will affect the distribution target and the amount of the fee. Before the changes take effect, the pending fee should be settled. Otherwise, it may lead to errors in settling the pending fees.

For example, if the current `affiliate` is Alice, the pending fee is 100. After changing the affiliate to Bob, this fee will be allocated to Bob instead of Alice during the rebalance.

**Recommendations**

Before adjusting the fee, the pending fee should be settled first.
