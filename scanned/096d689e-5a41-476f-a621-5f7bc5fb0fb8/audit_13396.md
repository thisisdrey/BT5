# Allowed users can directly transfer their share to RedeemManager

## Summary
This bug report is about an issue in two contracts, RedeemManager.1.sol and SharesManager.1.sol. It states that an allowed user can directly transfer its shares to the RedeemManager without requesting a redeem. This would cause the withdrawal stack to grow, since the redeem demand (2) which is calculated based on the RedeemManager 's share of LsETH increases. RedeemQueue would be untouched in this case. In case of an accidental mistake by a user, the locked shares can only be retrieved by a protocol update.

The recommendation for this issue is to make sure ShareManager would not allow users to directly transfer their shares to the RedeemManager unless the call to transferFrom is made by the RedeemManager. Liquid Collective has fixed this issue by introducing a storage variable RedeemDemand that would keep track of redeem demand for the redeem manager contract. However, it does not address the issue that shares can be directly transferred to the redeem manager by a user.
