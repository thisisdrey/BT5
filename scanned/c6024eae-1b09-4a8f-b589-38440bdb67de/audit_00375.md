# Revenue accounting drifts on outflows

## Summary
This bug report describes a problem in the way revenue is calculated and managed in a collateral manager. Due to the way pending revenue is updated, there is a risk of an incorrect calculation, which could lead to incorrect revenue collection and an underflow in the assets calculation. The report recommends enforcing a policy on how revenue should be handled, either by protecting it or allowing it to be spent, and suggests implementing a function to explicitly snapshot revenue during large outflows. The report has been acknowledged by the team, but the issue has not yet been fully resolved.

## Description
The collateral manager models revenue as the cumulative change in an ERC4626 vault's asset value since a stored snapshot and then subtracts that revenue from the total assets it reports. The view path uses this logic:

```solidity
uint256 revenue = _getRevenue(collateral, vault);
assets = _totalAssets(vault) + IERC20(collateral).balanceOf(address(this)) - revenue;
```

However, pending revenue is only updated when the manager itself performs a deposit, withdraw, withdrawRevenue, or convertRevenue. Collateral can leave the manager through redemption (the controller transfers from the manager), rebalancing, and swaps, and those flows do not refresh pending revenue or lastTotalAssets. 

Because collateral is fungible, these outflows can consume tokens that the accounting model still treats as revenue. This can make the computed revenue exceed the manager's actual holdings, which causes the assets calculation to underflow and revert, and can also cause revenue collection to fail after a prior read indicated revenue was available.

The result is a drift between accounting and reality that can break keeper automation and makes it easy for privileged operational flows to implicitly spend revenue that is intended to be separated from backing collateral. This creates a denial of service risk on accounting reads and revenue withdrawal flows and undermines the intended revenue separation model.

## Recommendation
Decide whether revenue must be protected or can be spent, then enforce that policy on-chain. If revenue should be protected, route outbound flows through the manager or add a pre-transfer hook that refreshes revenue and enforces a cap such as `spendingRevenue <= totalAssets + onHand - amountLeaving`.

If revenue can be spent, clamp revenue to available holdings in view logic to avoid underflow and to reflect the actual balance, and consider a sync function that explicitly snapshots revenue around large outflows.
