# Centralized Power / Security Mainly Depends on Off-Chain Component

## Summary
The bug report describes a design flaw in the current system where the off-chain component (admins) can withdraw all funds from the contract at once and an admin can unpause the protocol paused by another admin. This means that if an attacker manipulates one of the admins, they can have full control over the funds and the protocol. The report recommends two modifications to reduce the risk of centralized power: 1) ensuring that the amount distributed for a mission does not exceed the amount supplied and creators cannot manipulate rewards for other missions, and 2) only allowing admins to call `createDistribution()` with the right message signed by the creator or only allowing creators to call the function. However, the second option may allow creators to pull back their funds instead of paying them out to mission executors after completion.

## Details
**Update**
The dev team stated that this is intended by design and have decided not to change it.

**Description:** It is a rather centralized design; the reward withdrawal logic depends solely on Merkle roots provided by the off-chain component. Currently the design:

1.   Allows the off-chain component (i.e., the admins) to withdraw all the funds supplied by the creators for different missions from the contract at once.
2.   Allows an admin to unpause the protocol paused by another admin. This means if an attacker manages to manipulate one of the admins, there will be nothing remaining to stop them.

**Recommendation:** Two modifications should be made to reduce the risk of centralized power:

1.   `createDistribution()` should ensure that the amount to be distributed for a mission should not be greater than the supplied amount of that mission. And also, should make sure that a creator has no permission to manipulate the distribution of the rewards for the other missions that are not funded by themselves. We recommend making use of the `missions` mapping to keep track of the created missions, amounts and creators.
2.   a) Allow the admins to call `createDistribution()` only with the right message signed by the creator, or b) to allow only the creators to call this function. However, readers should be aware that the option (b) allows the creators to pull back the funds they supplied instead of paying them out to the mission executors after the mission has been completed.
