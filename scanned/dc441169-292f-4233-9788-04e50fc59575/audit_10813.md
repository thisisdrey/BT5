# [M-04] The `owner` of `AddressRegistry` can take over the protocol

## Summary
A bug has been found in the AddressRegistry contract that could potentially result in funds being stolen. This bug can only be exploited by a malicious or compromised owner of the contract. Currently, only the GOVERNANCE account has access to critical methods, but the owner of the AddressRegistry contract can change the GOVERNANCE account at any time. This means that the owner could set an address they control as the GOVERNANCE account and use it to gain control over protected methods, such as receiving all rewards in the protocol. To prevent this, it is recommended that the owner of the AddressRegistry contract be a Timelock contract controlled by a multi-sig or Governor smart contract. This will slow down any potential attacks, giving users time to stop using the protocol or withdraw their funds.

## Details
**Severity**

**Impact:**
High, as funds can be stolen

**Likelihood:**
Low, as it requires a malicious or a compromised `owner` of `AddressRegistry`

**Description**

Currently most contracts have critical methods only callable by the `GOVERNANCE` account. This account can be set or updated at anytime by the `owner` of the `AddressRegistry` contract, which allows him to set an address that he controls and then exploit all protected methods' functionality for his own gain, for example making him the only recipient of rewards in the protocol.

**Recommendations**

The `owner` of the `AddressRegistry` contract should be a `Timelock` contract that is controlled by a multi-sig or a `Governor` smart contract so that actual attacks will be done slow enough so that users will have time to possibly stop using the protocol or withdraw funds from it.
