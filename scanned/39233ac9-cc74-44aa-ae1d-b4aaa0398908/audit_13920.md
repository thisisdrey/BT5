# Incorrect Calculation For Exponential Curves

## Summary
This bug report discusses an issue with the Exponential pool in the Infinity Swap protocol. The problem is that the price decrement step is incorrectly implemented as V �� (1 − delta) instead of the correct operation V /(1+delta). This can lead to risks of price manipulation and potential loss of funds for pool owners. The report suggests a solution to use V /(1+delta) instead of V �� (1 − delta) to fix the issue. The report also includes code changes to resolve the bug, which have been implemented in the latest patches.

## Details
## Infinity Swap Audit 04 | Vulnerabilities

The inverse operation of \( V \times (1 + \delta) \) is \( V / (1 + \delta) \). The Exponential curves incorrectly implement the price decrement step as \( V \times (1 - \delta) \). Since \( V \times (1 + \delta) \times (1 - \delta) < V \), this exposes the Exponential pool to risks of price manipulation and may lead to loss of pool owner funds.

## Remediation

Use \( V / (1 + \delta) \) instead of \( V \times (1 - \delta) \).
