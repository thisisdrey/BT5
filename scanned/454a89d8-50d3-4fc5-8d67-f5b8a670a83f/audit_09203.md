# After trading on the curve in closeShort and applying the fees, spot price is not check to make sure it is still below 1

## Summary
The report highlights a bug in the code related to calculating interest for a trading system. The bug occurs when trading on a curve, where the spot price may become negative. This is due to an incorrect inequality check, which does not take into account the added fees and reserves. The recommendation is to fix this by ensuring that the spot price is always less than 1. The bug has been fixed in a recent update and has been verified.

## Details
## Severity: Medium Risk

## Context
- HyperdriveShort.sol#L519-L538
- HyperdriveShort.sol#L570

## Description
In `_calculateCloseShort`, one checks the following inequality to make sure the non-matured portion of \( y \) (which is \( \text{tr} \) when given back to the pool) does not bear negative interest:

\[
(z - \delta + z_{\text{curve}}) y - \text{tr} \cdot y \cdot t_s < 1 - c(1 - p_{\text{spot}}) \cdot 1
\]

The far right inequality should be right since we need to have checked that before trading on the curve, we were in the region with \( p_{\text{spot}} < 1 \). Thus, the negative interest inequality would guarantee that the point \( (z - \delta + z, y - \text{tr} \cdot y) \) would also have a spot price of less than 1.

But after trading on the curve, we add the LP/curve fees minus the governance fee to the share:

\[
(z - \delta, y) \nrightarrow (z - \delta + z, y - \text{tr} \cdot y) \nrightarrow (z - \delta + z + (1 - \epsilon_g) f_c, y - \text{tr} \cdot y)
\]

Since the share reserves have increased when adding the pool fees, one does not necessarily know that the spot price at this point is still less than 1:

\[
(z - \delta + z_{\text{curve}} + (1 - \epsilon_g) f_c) y - \text{tr} \cdot y \cdot t_s
\]

## Recommendation
In `_calculateCloseShort`, when adding the curve fees, we still need to make sure the point we end up with has a spot price of less than 1:

\[
(z - \delta + z_{\text{curve}} + (1 - \epsilon_g) f_c) y - \text{tr} \cdot y \cdot t_s < 1
\]

This should be equivalent to, if not considering the error introduced in `pow`, to the following:

\[
(z - \delta + z_{\text{curve}} + (1 - \epsilon_g) f_c) y - \text{tr} \cdot y < 1
\]

Or even better, to avoid division errors and costs:

\[
(z - \delta + z_{\text{curve}} + (1 - \epsilon_g) f_c) < y - \text{tr} \cdot y
\]
