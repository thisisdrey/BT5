# ETH Can Get Locked in Contracts

## Summary
The bug report states that there are multiple instances in the code where ETH can become locked. This means that there is a possibility for ETH to become inaccessible or unusable in certain parts of the code. Two specific instances are mentioned, one in the `multicall` function of Multicall.sol and the other in the `constructor` function of PanopticHelper.sol. The report also mentions that the Panoptic team has acknowledged the issue but it has not been fixed yet. They have stated that this is intended and the functions are marked as `payable` for a gas cost reduction. They also mention that there is no reason for end users to send ETH along with their calls and if they do, it will have no effect on the protocol's operations and will be equivalent to burning ETH.

## Details
There are multiple occurrences throughout the codebase where ETH can become locked. For instance:


* In the `multicall` function of Multicall.sol
* In the `constructor` function of PanopticHelper.sol


***Update:** Acknowledged, not fixed. The Panoptic team stated:*



> This is intended. The functions are marked `payable` for a gas cost reduction, and there is no reason for end users to send ETH along with their calls. If they decide to do so, it is equivalent to burning ETH and has no effect on the operations of the protocol.
