# Loss of Precision Due to Division Before Multiplication

## Summary
The report discusses a bug that was found and fixed in the `Utils.sol` file. The bug was caused by division before multiplication, which resulted in a loss of precision when working with integer numbers. The bug was identified in several functions, including `_convertETHWeiToUSDWei()`, `_convertUSDWeiToETHWei()`, `_isClose()`, and `_getETHPriceInUSD()`. The recommendation is to perform multiplication before division to reduce the loss of precision, and to consider adding more zeros for greater precision in the `_isClose()` function. The bug has been fixed in the latest update.

## Details
**Update**
Addressed in `eb19b5fbf591ae5db63151c060dadad2725916b0` The client rewrote `_getETHPriceInUSD()`, `_convertETHWeiToUSDWei()`, and `__convertUSDWeiToETHWei()` to adjust for loss in precision.

**File(s) affected:**`Utils.sol`

**Description:** Division before multiplication may result in a loss of precision when the operations are carried over integer numbers. We have identified several instances where this is the case:

*   `Utils._convertETHWeiToUSDWei()`: First performs division in `_getETHPriceInUSD` and multiplies by `amountInETH` after returned.
*   `Utils._convertUSDWeiToETHWei()`: Performs a division in `_getETHPriceInUSD` and subsequently divides by its return value. Can be rewritten as multiplication then division.
*   `Utils._isClose()`: Performs a division of `absDifference` by `originalAmount` prior to multiplying by `100`.
*   `Utils._getETHPriceInUSD()`: Performs a truncation of the USD cents.

**Recommendation:** To reduce the loss of precision, it is generally advised to perform multiplication before division operations. In addition for `Utils._isClose()`, consider padding the `absDifference` with additional zeros for greater precision.
