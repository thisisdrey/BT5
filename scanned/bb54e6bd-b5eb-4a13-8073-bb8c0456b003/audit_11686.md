# Attacker Can Break Buying Nfts From Market

## Summary
The report discusses a bug that has been fixed in the ParticleExchange contract. The bug involved the incorrect calculation of ETH and WETH balances during a market trade, which could lead to an error being triggered and prevent users from buying NFTs. The bug has been fixed by accounting for the total balance of ETH and WETH at the start of the trade.

## Details
**Update**
The team fixed the issue in commit `bea3d4d3da201443ec3df744b204a1e6155ca8a2` by adding the initial WETH balance to the initial ETH balance before making the comparison.

**File(s) affected:**`ParticleExchange.sol`

**Description:** The `_execBuyNftMarket()` keeps track of the balance of ETH before and after the market trade in order to ensure the correct amount of ETH or WETH is used in the trade. Whether or not WETH is used as payment for the trade, the contract's entire balance of WETH will be converted to ETH after the trade. Therefore, if the contract holds any WETH before the trade, it will be converted back to ETH and invalidate the strict inequality check and trigger `Errors.InvalidNFTBuy()`.

**Exploit Scenario:** 1. Attacker sends 1 wei of WETH to `ParticleExchange`.

1.   No user can call `buyNftFromMarket()` until someone calls `sellNftToMarketPull()` or `sellNftToMarketPush()`.

**Recommendation:** Account for the total ETH and WETH balance of the contract at the start of `_execBuyNftFromMarket()`.
