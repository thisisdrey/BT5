# Adherence To Specification: `BIT` Holders Might Get Less `MNT`

## Summary
The bug report discusses an issue with the `MantleTokenMigrator` contract in the `mantle-token-contracts` repository. The proposal in BitDAO states a 1:1 conversion ratio between `BIT` and `MNT` tokens, but the contract may not guarantee this ratio due to the use of constants and conversion calculations. The report recommends removing these or updating the proposal and related documentation. The bug has been marked as fixed by the client and the issue has been addressed in a specific commit.

## Details
**Update**
Fixed in commit [b187163](https://github.com/mantlenetworkio/mantle-token-contracts/commit/b18716323f4054120856247ec5e3c28aa3bdb5da) by removing the numerator and denominator functionality and minting `MNT` tokens 1:1 to input `BIT` tokens, as described in the specification.

**Update**
Marked as "Fixed" by the client. Addressed in: `b18716323f4054120856247ec5e3c28aa3bdb5da`.

**File(s) affected:**`MantleTokenMigrator.sol`

**Description:** The [proposal in BitDAO](https://snapshot.org/#/bitdao.eth/proposal/0x950dac4d5715b8aa8eab29c484b1c9dd0eed161141262b0425874f65be4d9f8e) states:

> “The one-way token conversion ratio shall be set at 1 $BIT token to 1 $MNT token”.

However, the `MantleTokenMigrator` contract computes the ratio based on `TOKEN_CONVERSION_NUMERATOR` and `TOKEN_CONVERSION_DENOMINATOR`, possibly not guaranteeing the 1:1 ratio.

**Recommendation:** We recommend removing such constants and conversion calculations or updating the proposal and other related documentation.
