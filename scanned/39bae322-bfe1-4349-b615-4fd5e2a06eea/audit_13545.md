# More than the Premium Amount Can Be Spent when a Premium Is Paid

## Summary
The client has marked a bug as "Fixed" in three files: `LibSimplePolicy.sol`, `LibFeeRouter.sol`, and `LibEntity.sol`. The bug was related to payment of a premium using the function `LibFeeRouter._payPremiumCommissions()`. The bug allowed for the possibility of spending more than the premium amount or the payment failing due to insufficient funds. The bug was caused by not checking if the sum of policy-level and system-level commissionBasisPoints was greater than 10000. The recommendation is to add checks to ensure the total amount of commissions is never greater than 10000 and to record the full amount of commissions paid in the event `PremiumCommissionsPaid`. Additionally, it is suggested to set a maximum proportion for policy-level and system-level commissions and to not change these proportions once initialized.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Fixed" by the client. Addressed in: `ec3937090a05bc4c430a77f6a18924ce70590bf6`. All three items were fixed. 

Note: we communicated to the team that the operator `<=` in the require statement at L78 in the contract `LibEntity.sol` could be replaced with the operator `==`.

**File(s) affected:**`LibSimplePolicy.sol`, `LibFeeRouter.sol`, `LibEntity.sol`

**Description:** The payment of a premium can be done with the function `LibFeeRouter._payPremiumCommissions()`. Premium commissions can be defined at 2 levels:

1.   at the level of the policy: a list of `commissionReceivers` is defined, each having a defined `commissionBasisPoints` fee assigned;
2.   at the level of the system: three actors (NaymsLtd, NDF, STM) have dedicated `commissionBasisPoints` fees assigned: `premiumCommissionNaymsLtdBP`, `premiumCommissionNDFBP`, `premiumCommissionSTMBP`;

For the policy level, the function `LibEntity._validateSimplePolicyCreation()` makes sure that the sum of all commissionBasisPoints is `< 10000`. For the system level, the function `InitDiamond.initialize()` is used to initially define the three variables. Also, the function `LibFeeRouter._updatePolicyCommissionsBasisPoints()` is used to update the three variables and make sure that the sum is `< 10000`.

However, none of the operations to create a policy and update system-level premium commissions makes sure that there is no policy in the system for which the sum of policy-level and system-level `commissionBasisPoints` is `> 10000`. In that situation, more than the premium amount to be paid could be spent by the entity. Alternatively, the payment could also fail if there is no sufficient amount to be spent.

The likelihood is low, as only `sysAdmin` and `sysManager` can execute these functions.

Also, in the same function, the event `PremiumCommissionsPaid` only records the sum of system-level commissions that have been paid. It does not record what has been paid for the policy-level commissions.

**Recommendation:** Consider adding checks to make sure that the total amount of premium commissions (system-level + policy-level) is never `> 10000`. Also, make sure that the event `PremiumCommissionsPaid` records the full amount of commissions that have been paid.

Note that while it will be easy to check during the creation of a simple policy whether the total commission satisfies the invariant, it will be inefficient to check the invariant against all active policies if the admin decides to update the global policy commission basis points. In this case, an option could be to set a maximum proportion of commission it can receive at system level and at policy level, say `A%` and `B%` respectively, such that `A + B = 100`. Furthermore, the proportions should never be changed once initialized.
