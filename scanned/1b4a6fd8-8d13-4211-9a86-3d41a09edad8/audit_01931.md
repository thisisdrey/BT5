# Ongoing or Imminent Sales Can Be Arbitrarily Updated

## Summary
The client has marked a bug as "Fixed" in a recent update. The issue was related to the `early_purchase::update_sale::handler()` function, which allowed certain users to modify important parameters in a sale even after it had started. This could result in buyers paying a different price or facing different purchasing conditions than expected. To prevent this, it is recommended to implement a parameter lock before the sale starts to protect buyers and ensure predictability.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Fixed" by the client. Addressed in: `15f4cc0e3fe47cc36652505b4686d1cd3e6eb7bd`. The client provided the following explanation:

> A check was added in update_sale verifying that the sale has not started before changes are made

**File(s) affected:**`programs/early-purchase/src/instructions/update_sale.rs`

**Description:** The `early_purchase::update_sale::handler()` function permits `Guardians` with `update_sale` permissions to modify critical parameters such as `sale.purchase_mint`, `sale.payment_mint`, `sale.guard_purchases` and so on, even if the `sale.is_start_time_reached()`is `true`.

Buyers typically make purchase decisions based on the observed state of the sale. If these parameters can be altered while the sale is active or just moments before a buyer's transaction is processed, a buyer might inadvertently pay a different price (potentially higher) or face different purchasing conditions than they initially expected.

The`purchase_tokens`instruction calculates the cost dynamically at the time of execution based on the current`Sale`state. This could lead to user frustration, loss of trust, and scenarios where buyers feel deceived if, for instance, the token price effectively increases due to a mid-sale update.

**Recommendation:** Consider enabling a parameter lock before the sale starts, which prevents Guardians from updating critical sale properties to protect buyers and ensure predictability.
