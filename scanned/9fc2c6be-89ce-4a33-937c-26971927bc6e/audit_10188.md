# M-4: High-risk actions aren't all covered by the existing checks

## Summary
This bug report discusses a vulnerability in the Olympus on-chain governance system. The issue was found by a group of individuals and involves the lack of checks for high-risk actions such as changing the list of high-risk operations or migrating kernels. These actions are not currently marked as high-risk, which can allow an attacker to brick the protocol or lower the threshold for high-risk actions. The report includes a code snippet and a recommendation to add these operations to the high-risk category. The issue was found through a manual review and has been fixed by the development team. The severity of this issue is considered medium and there are duplicates of this issue. The escalation status of this issue has been resolved successfully.

## Found by 
IllIllI, LTDingZhen, cawfree, ck
## Summary

Things such as changing the list of high risk operations, or migrating kernels are not counted as high risk, even though they are high-risk


## Vulnerability Detail

High risk modules are checked against a mapping, but the changing of values within the mapping is not marked as high risk.

In addition, the `MigrateKernel` action is not protected, even though it can [brick the protocol](https://github.com/sherlock-audit/2024-01-olympus-on-chain-governance/blob/main/bophades/src/Kernel.sol#L338)


## Impact

Allows an attacker to brick the protocol with a low threshold, or to remove the high-risk modules from the list of high risk modules, resulting in a lower threshold
Violates invariant of high-risk actions needing to be behind a higher quorum
