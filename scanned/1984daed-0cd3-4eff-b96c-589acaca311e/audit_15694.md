# Negative rebase of stETH could prevent a round from ending

## Summary
This bug report is about an issue with the STETHVault.sol, a contract used by the Pods Finance yield contracts. The issue was that when a round ended, the amount of underlying assets currently in the vault was subtracted from the amount of assets the vault contained in the previous round. This calculation assumed a positive yield, but the underlying asset stETH was able to rebase in both a positive and negative direction due to the potential for slashing, which could cause an underflow. This underflow would prevent the controller from being able to end the round until the totalAssets was greater than lastRoundAssets.

To resolve the issue, it was recommended to place the logic that calculates and transfers any investment yield generated by the vault in an if statement that only occurs when totalAssets is greater than lastRoundAssets. This would allow any yield generated from the options strategy to still be transferred to the vault and prevent the accrued interest arithmetic from reverting. The issue was resolved in PR#100, with commit a756b4d8ead7fce109e9daf51c218eb952454487 being the last one added.

## Details
When a round ends, the amount of underlying assets currently in the vault is [subtracted](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/blob/c4b401ce674c24798de5f9d02c82e466ee0a2600/contracts/vaults/STETHVault.sol#L97) from the amount of assets the vault contained in the previous round. This calculation assumes a positive yield, but the underlying asset stETH is able to rebase in both a positive and negative direction due to the potential for slashing. In the case where Lido is slashed, `totalAssets` can be less than `lastRoundAssets`. Consequently, the subtraction would cause an underflow, which would prevent the controller from being able to end the round until `totalAssets` is greater than `lastRoundAssets`.


Consider placing the logic that [calculates and transfers](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/blob/c4b401ce674c24798de5f9d02c82e466ee0a2600/contracts/vaults/STETHVault.sol#L97-L103) any investment yield generated by the vault in an `if` statement that only occurs when `totalAssets` is greater than `lastRoundAssets`. This allows any yield generated from the options strategy to still be transferred to the vault and prevents the accrued interest arithmetic from reverting.


**Update:** *Resolved in [PR#100](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/pull/100), with commit `a756b4d8ead7fce109e9daf51c218eb952454487` being the last one added.*
