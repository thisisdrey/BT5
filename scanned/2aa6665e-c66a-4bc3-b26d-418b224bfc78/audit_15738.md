# Rounding issue can lead to lacking funds in the contract

## Summary
A bug report has been submitted that when the variable 'arbitrated' is set to true, the arbitrator fee is deducted from both the buyer and seller splits, but the two fees do not sum up to the total fee. This is further compounded by the fact that the arbitrator fee is calculated again with the total amount in the `calculatePayment()` function. 

To illustrate the issue, it is assumed that the arbitrator fee is 1000 (10%) and the buyer and seller splits are 4999 and 5001 respectively. The arbitrator fee from the buyer should be 4999 * 10% = 499, and the fee from the seller should be 500. The sum of the two fees should be 999, but it is not equal to the total fee of 1000. 

Additionally, when the fee is taken from the buyer and seller splits, the total of the two splits is 10001, which is greater than the total amount of 10000. 

The recommendation is to consider returning the arbitrator with the remaining amount without calculating it using the `arbitratorFee`.

## Details
**Description:** 

In case `arbitrated = True`,arbitratorFee will be deducted from both buyer and seller splits. However they did not be sum up but after all,in `calculatePayment()`,arbitratorFee is calculated again with total amount.

Scenario (for simplicity,let's just assume there is only arbitrator fee)

1. arbitratorFee = 1000 (10%),split[Buyer]= 4999,split[seller]= 5001

arbitratorFeeFromBuyer = 4999 \*10%= 499,arbitratorFeeFromSeller = 500

sum = 999 != 1000 ??

2. After take fee split[Buyer]= 4500,split[seller]= 4501
3. Total splits = split[Buyer]+ split[seller]+ arbitratorFee = 10001> 10000

**Recommendations:** 

Consider returning arbitrator with the remaining amount without calculating it using `arbitratorFee`
