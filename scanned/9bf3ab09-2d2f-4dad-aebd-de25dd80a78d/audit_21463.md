# [H03] Malicious owner can execute Denial of Service attacks

## Summary
A bug report was filed for the `Option` contract which is part of the Primitive Protocol. This contract inherits the pausing feature from the OpenZeppelin ContractŌĆÖs `Pausable` contract. The `Option` contract has a `kill` function that can pause or unpause the contract, and only the `factory` address can call it. The `owner` account of the `Registry` contract can also call the `kill` function and trigger a pause. Pausing the `Option` contract does not have a timelock mechanism in place, and the private key for the administratorŌĆÖs account is expected to be in control of the Primitive team. Pausing only affects the `mint` and `exercise` functions, and if the private key controlling the owner account is compromised, the attacker can indefinitely prevent new options from being minted, and all issued options from being effectively exercised.

To mitigate this attack vector, the Primitive team removed the entire pausing functionality from the `Option` contract by removing the inheritance from `Pausable`, the `kill` function and the `whenNotPaused` modifier. This does not follow the recommendation, but it does mitigate the described attack vector. To prevent similar vulnerabilities in the future, the Primitive team should add extensive, user-friendly documentation related to the implemented pausing mechanism. This should explain under which circumstances the system is to be paused or unpaused, what are the specific consequences of pausing over the systemŌĆÖs mechanics, and who is allowed to trigger this feature. Additionally, the Primitive team should devise and implement mechanisms that favor decentralization, giving users the possibility to exit the system before its behavior changes.

## Details
The [`Option` contract](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol) inherits the pausing feature from the [OpenZeppelin ContractŌĆÖs `Pausable` contract](https://github.com/OpenZeppelin/openzeppelin-contracts/blob/v3.1.0/contracts/utils/Pausable.sol). In each `Option` contract, only the [`factory` address](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L84) is allowed to call the [`kill` function](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L83) that can effective pause or unpause the contract. Following the access control chain, it can be noted that the [`owner` account](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/applications/Registry.sol#L92) of the `Registry` contract can call the `kill` function and trigger a pause. It must be highlighted that pausing and unpausing the `Option` contract is an immediate action, since there is no timelock mechanism in place. Moreover, the private key for the administratorŌĆÖs account is expected to be in control of the Primitive team, so users should fully trust them to execute a pause / unpause action under the right circumstances.


Pausing only affects the [`mint`](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L149) and the [`exercise`](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L188) functions. If a the private key controlling the owner account is compromised, the attacker can indefinitely prevent new options from being minted, and all issued options from being effectively exercised.


This kind of simple fail-safe mechanism are reasonable for early versions of projects under development and testing. However, they become more relevant as projects launch to mainnet, gain importance in the space, or users start depositing large sums of money into them.


In the short term, consider adding extensive, user-friendly, documentation related to the implemented pausing mechanism. The documentation should highlight under which circumstances the system is to be paused or unpaused, what are the specific consequences of pausing over the systemŌĆÖs mechanics, and who is allowed to trigger this feature. For future versions of the protocol, it is advisable to start devising and implementing mechanisms that favor decentralization, giving users the possibility to exit the system before its behavior changes.


**Update:** *Fixed in [PR #20](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/pull/20). The Primitive team has removed the entire pausing functionality from the `Option` contract, by removing the inheritance from `Pausable`, the `kill` function and the `whenNotPaused` modifier. Note that these changes are not inline with our recommendation, even though they do mitigate the described attack vector.*
