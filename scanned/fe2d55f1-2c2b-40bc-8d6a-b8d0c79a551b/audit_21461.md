# [M02] Exercises may avoid fee payments

## Summary
The Primitive Protocol is a system that intends to charge fees when an option is exercised. The fees are calculated as a percentage of the amount of underlying tokens that the Option sends out in the exercise and are expected to be paid by the caller in strike tokens. However, callers can avoid paying the fees if they exercise an extremely low amount of tokens, such as less than 1000. This is because the fee is calculated using Solidity’s integer division which rounds down to zero any value lower than 1, and most tokens have 18 decimals. This issue becomes more important as the number of decimals of the underlying token shrinks.

To ensure that fees are always charged, it is recommended to implement validations in the `exercise` function of the `Option` contract so that the transaction reverts in case the calculated fee is zero. Alternatively, the behavior should be clearly documented and tested to prevent unexpected outcomes. This issue has since been fixed in PR #16 and the concept of fee has been removed from the system altogether.

## Details
The Primitive protocol intends to charge fees [when an option is exercised](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L224-L225). These fees are [calculated as a percentage of the amount of underlying tokens](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L225) the `Option` sends out in the exercise, and are expected to be paid by the caller [in strike tokens](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L233).


While all exercises are expected to pay fees, callers can avoid paying them by exercising extremely low amount of tokens. In particular, in all exercises where the amount of underlying tokens to send out (which is a [user-controlled parameter](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L181)) is lower than 1000, no fees will be charged. This is due to the fact that [the fee is calculated using Solidity’s integer division](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/blob/78a8e64b7618e9199203ab84042876c580ae1e90/packages/primitive-contracts/contracts/option/primitives/Option.sol#L225), which rounds down to zero any value lower than 1.


Considering most tokens have 18 decimals, exercises with amounts lower than 1000 could be considered unlikely, since it might not be profitable for anyone (accounting for gas costs) to exercise such amount. However, the scenario described becomes more important as the number of decimals of the underlying token shrinks. For example, traders using tokens with less decimals such as USDC (which has 6), are more likely to take advantage of this issue to avoid fee payments.


Should exercises be expected to always charge a fee, consider implementing the necessary validations in the `exercise` function of the `Option` contract so that the transaction reverts in case the calculated fee is zero. Otherwise, consider clearly documenting and testing this behavior to prevent unexpected outcomes.


**Update:** *Fixed in [PR #16](https://github.com/primitivefinance/primitive-protocol/pull/16). The concept of fee has been removed from the system altogether.*
