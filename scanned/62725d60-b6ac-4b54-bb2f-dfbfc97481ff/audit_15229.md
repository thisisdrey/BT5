# Burn percentage applies retroactively

## Summary
This bug report is about the Optimistic Asserter contract in the UMA Protocol. When creating an assertion, the minimum bond of the configurable burn percentage depends on the current value, and it is expected that the reward for asserters and disputers would be the bond reduced by the `burnedBondPercentage` at the time the assertion was originally made. However, the burn percentage is not cached, resulting in the actual reward being reduced by the most recent value at the time of settlement.

The suggested solution is to cache the burn percentage and use it consistently throughout the life of an assertion. However, the UMA team has decided not to make this change as they believe that it would be too costly and that it could lead to potential abuse. They also stated that adding an additional property to an assertion would require additional data structures or other refactors that they don't want to do at this time.

## Details
When creating an assertion, the minimum bond [depends on the current value](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/fed1c5677b5e764eb0b3e59817941c129e6c26f7/packages/core/contracts/optimistic-asserter/implementation/OptimisticAsserter.sol#L360) of the [configurable](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/fed1c5677b5e764eb0b3e59817941c129e6c26f7/packages/core/contracts/optimistic-asserter/implementation/OptimisticAsserter.sol#L74) burn percentage. Asserters and disputers would reasonably expect their reward to be the bond reduced by the `burnedBondPercentage` at the time the assertion was originally made. However, in contrast to [other configurable parameters](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/fed1c5677b5e764eb0b3e59817941c129e6c26f7/packages/core/contracts/optimistic-asserter/implementation/OptimisticAsserter.sol#L179), the burn percentage is not cached, resulting in the actual reward being reduced by the most recent value [at the time of settlement](https://github.com/UMAprotocol/protocol/blob/fed1c5677b5e764eb0b3e59817941c129e6c26f7/packages/core/contracts/optimistic-asserter/implementation/OptimisticAsserter.sol#L272).


Consider caching the burn percentage and using it consistently throughout the life of an assertion.


***Update:** Acknowledged, not resolved. The UMA team stated:*



> *We’ve decided to not make this change. The implications of the issue presented here is that the voters vote to change the bond burn percentage after an assertion is disputed with the goal of stealing it from the disputer (for example setting the bond burn percentage to 100% after an assertion is made). We feel that this is a non-issue as the reputational damage of the DVM doing this would destroy the project and the cost to the protocol would be more than the value extracted from taking the reward.*
> 
> 
> *Additionally, we are at the stack depth for the assertion object and adding an additional property to an assertion (bond burn percentage at assertion time) would require additional data structures or other refactors to accommodate this that we don’t want to do at this time.*
> 
>
