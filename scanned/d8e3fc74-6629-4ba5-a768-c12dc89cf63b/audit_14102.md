# Technical Documentation and Tests Are Severely Lacking

## Summary
The report discusses issues with the documentation and testing of a project called Level Finance. The codebase is large and interconnected, but there is no proper documentation to explain the purpose of each contract and how they work together. This has made it difficult for the auditors to understand the system and conduct their tests. The lack of tests also increases the risk of errors in the code. The auditors recommend that the Level Finance team thoroughly document their smart contracts and develop test suites with full coverage to ensure proper functioning of the system.

**Description:** The protocol is overall underdocumented and underspecified. With a codebase so large and interconnected, there is a distinct need for documentation that can explain the purpose of each contract and how they fit together. Moreover, there is no technical specification to describe how the system should work as a whole.

In the absence of such documentation, client-authored test suites are often used in order to arrive at something resembling a list of functional requirements. However, given the almost-entire absence of tests for the two audited repositories, this was not possible during the audit.

In light of the above, communication with the client was pursued extensively in order to clarify design decisions and intended behaviour. However, this level of communication rarely suffices to alleviate problems caused by a lack of documentation. As a result, the audit was significantly impaired.

Furthermore, the lack of tests increases operational risk for Level Finance, especially given the layered contract calls and interactions. Proper configuration is key for ensuring intended functionality, and yet there are no post-deployment checks.

**Recommendation:** We strongly recommend the Level Finance team thoroughly document their smart contracts and the overall intended manner of operation of their system. We also recommend that test suites with full coverage are developed in order to ensure proper functioning of the smart contracts in the abstract. Finally, integration tests would ensure proper functioning on-chain.
