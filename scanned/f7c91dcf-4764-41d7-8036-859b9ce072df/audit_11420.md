# Ownership-Based Function Controls

## Summary
This bug report is about a problem with certain functions in a contract, specifically `enableTrading`, `removeLimits`, and `updateSwapEnabled`. These functions are currently controlled by the owner of the contract, but if the owner's address is compromised, it could lead to serious consequences. An attacker could stop trading, make important changes to the contract, or even steal funds. The recommendation is to add additional security measures, such as requiring multiple people or a decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) to approve these functions, instead of just relying on the owner's control. This will help prevent potential attacks and protect the contract and its users.

## Details
**Severity**: Low

**Status**: Acknowledged

**Description**: 

Functions like `enableTrading`, `removeLimits`, and `updateSwapEnabled` are controlled solely based on contract ownership. If the owner's address is compromised, it could lead to catastrophic consequences.
If the owner's address is compromised, an attacker could halt trading, change critical contract parameters, or even drain funds.

**Recommendation**: 

Implement a multi-signature or DAO-based control for critical functions rather than relying solely on the `onlyOwner` modifier.
