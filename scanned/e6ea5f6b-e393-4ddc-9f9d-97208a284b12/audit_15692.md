# Phantom permit functions callable from child vault

## Summary
The bug report is about the BaseVault contract, which implements a mintWithPermit and depositWithPermit function, even though its underlying asset is stETH which does not have a permit function. The Pods Finance team kept this functionality as they may create other vaults in the future with underlying assets that do contain permit functions. It was suggested that the calls to permit in the STETHVault contract should be explicitly reverted when called. This would provide an accurate error message, and prevent any potential issues arising from future upgrades to the stETH contract, or if the underlying asset contains phantom permit functions. The bug was resolved in PR#123, with the last commit being fd3ca22cedc414f9e6f5fbed34ad725ca509bff2.

## Details
In the previous audit, we described an issue where the [`BaseVault`](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/blob/c4b401ce674c24798de5f9d02c82e466ee0a2600/contracts/vaults/BaseVault.sol) implements a [`mintWithPermit`](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/blob/c4b401ce674c24798de5f9d02c82e466ee0a2600/contracts/vaults/BaseVault.sol#L152) and [`depositWithPermit`](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/blob/c4b401ce674c24798de5f9d02c82e466ee0a2600/contracts/vaults/BaseVault.sol#L124) function, even though the vault’s underlying asset is intended to be [stETH](https://etherscan.io/address/0x47ebab13b806773ec2a2d16873e2df770d130b50#code#F10#L50), which does not have a `permit` function. The Pods Finance team acknowledged this issue and explained that they kept this functionality because they may create other vaults in the future with underlying assets that do contain `permit` functions.


In this case, consider overriding the functions that call `permit` in the [`STETHVault`](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/blob/c4b401ce674c24798de5f9d02c82e466ee0a2600/contracts/vaults/STETHVault.sol) to explicitly revert when called. In the `STETHVault` contract, the calls to `mintWithPermit` and `depositWithPermit` will currently have their transaction reverted due to the stETH contract’s [fallback](https://etherscan.io/address/0x47ebab13b806773ec2a2d16873e2df770d130b50#code#F1#L279) function. However, this both provides a potentially inaccurate error message and is subject to different behavior if the stETH contract undergoes an upgrade. Further, tokens containing [phantom permit functions](https://media.dedaub.com/phantom-functions-and-the-billion-dollar-no-op-c56f062ae49f) are known to exist, which would not revert on failure. Setting a precedent of explicitly reverting when calling unsupported `permit` functions can help ensure future vaults do not suffer from calling phantom permits on their underlying assets.


**Update:** *Resolved in [PR#123](https://github.com/pods-finance/yield-contracts/pull/123), with commit `fd3ca22cedc414f9e6f5fbed34ad725ca509bff2` being the last one added.*
