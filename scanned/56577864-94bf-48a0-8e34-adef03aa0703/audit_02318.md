# Forefitted Burnt `tgUSD` and `stgUSD`

## Summary
The client has fixed an issue in their code that could have caused users to lose their tgUSD or stgUSD tokens without receiving a refund. This issue could occur if a user called the `op::burn` operation in their `jetton-wallet` contract with an invalid or missing forward payload. This would cause the `OP_EXTRA_BURN_INFO` operation in the `tgusd-engine` or `tgusd-staking` contract to fail and throw an error, without refunding the user their tokens. The client has provided a temporary solution for this issue, but it could potentially break the system. They have also provided two recommended solutions to prevent this issue from happening in the future.

## Details
**Update**
The client mitigated the issue in commit `1302e84c9241c970c8d9c33045632034031dcd4b` and provided the following explanation:

> Our definition of a refund applies only to errors that are reasonably likely to occur accidentally (e.g., expired orders, duplicate redemptions). For cases involving malicious trial-and-error or improperly formatted payloads (where users are expected to follow the SDK or documented message formats), we intentionally do not issue refunds to increase the cost of misbehavior. In response to this specific issue raised during the audit, we have adjusted the logic as follows:
> 
> 
> *   We implement refunds if the redeemer is not whitelisted.
> *   However, for improperly formatted payloads, we maintain the policy of not issuing refunds. A similar approach has also been applied to [TORCH-8](https://certificate.quantstamp.com/full/torch-finance/9edd725c-737d-4273-b2ca-6e65ce7b7575/index.html#findings-qs8): Invalid Collateral Deposits and tgUSD Stakes Require Manual Intervention. We remain open to hearing your recommendations to further optimize our handling of such cases.

**File(s) affected:**`contracts/tgusd-engine/main.tolk`, `contracts/tgusd-staking/main.tolk`, `contracts/jetton/jetton-wallet.fc`, `contracts/jetton/jetton-minter.fc`

**Description:** If a user were to call the `op::burn` operation in their tgUSD `jetton-wallet` contract with either:

1.   No forward payload.
2.   A forward payload that does not include the `OP_REQUEST_REDEEM` operation.
3.   A `OP_REQUEST_REDEEM` forward payload with a malformed or invalid order.
4.   A `OP_REQUEST_REDEEM` forward payload with a valid order, but the user is not in the `redeemWhitelistDict`.

The `OP_EXTRA_BURN_INFO` operation in the `tgusd-engine` contract will fail and throw an error without refunding the user their tgUSD.

Similarly, if a user were to call the `op::burn` operation in their stgUSD `jetton-wallet` contract with no forward payload, the `OP_EXTRA_BURN_INFO` operation in the `tgusd-staking` contract will fail and throw an error without refunding the user their stgUSD.

Due to a lack of logic in the `jetton-minter` contract in the `op::call_to` operation to force mint tgUSD or stgUSD, the issue is exacerbated as the `tgusd-engine` or `tgusd-staking` admin will have no way of refunding the user's burnt tgUSD or stgUSD without breaking the system altogether.

Technically, in an indirect, multi-step process, the `tgusd-engine` or `tgusd-staking` admin can do the following:

1.   Halt the `tgusd-engine` or `tgusd-staking` contract.
2.   Update the `jetton-minter` admin via the `OP_CHANGE_JETTON_MASTER_ADMIN` operation to a temporary address.
3.   Manually mint tgUSD or stgUSD back to the user.

However, since the `jetton-minter` contract has a two-step ownership transfer process, and the `tgusd-engine` and `tgusd-staking` contracts do not have logic to claim the admin role, the entire system would break if the above steps were executed.

**Recommendation:** Consider one of the following recommendations to prevent refitted burnt tgUSD or stgUSD tokens:

1.   When the `OP_EXTRA_BURN_INFO` operation in the `tgusd-engine` contract is executed, bundle all of the potentially reverting checks and execute `refundByMint()` if any of the checks fail. This approach would require refactoring the `require*()` checks to return a boolean instead of throwing an error. Alternatively, wrap the body of the `OP_EXTRA_BURN_INFO` in a `try` block and execute `refundByMint()` in the `catch` block.
2.   Create a global dictionary in the `tgusd-engine` contract that tracks invalid tgUSD burns by storing the `jettonSender` and the amount of tgUSD they have burned. When the `OP_EXTRA_BURN_INFO` operation is executed, and one of the checks fails, add the `jettonAmount` to the dictionary for the `jettonSender`. Further, add an operation in the `tgusd-engine` contract that allows the `admin` to mint tgUSD back to any user in the dictionary up to their tracked `burnBalance`. Keep in mind that this approach could be a target for denial-of-service or storage cost attacks, as malicious users could burn a small amount of tgUSD with an invalid forward payload from numerous wallets to increase the storage size of the dictionary. Additionally, this solution would result in manual intervention from the `tgusd-engine` admin.
