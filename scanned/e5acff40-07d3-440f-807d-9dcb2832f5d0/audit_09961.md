# Double Accounting of `commissionAmount` in `swapEth()`

## Summary
The client has marked a bug as "fixed" in the `ReachAffiliateDistribution.sol` file. In the `swapEth()` function, a portion of `_ethAmount` is transferred to the main distribution contract but is not accounted for in the subsequent swap for `REACH` tokens. This can cause issues with the contract's accounting and may prevent some users from claiming their rewards. The recommendation is to change the `value` in the swap function to properly account for the amount transferred out.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Fixed" by the client. Addressed in: `d44be2d`. The client provided the following explanation:

> An update to the method has been provided. Commission amount is now computed offchain and sent to the method as input. Comission and ethToSwap are mutually exclusive and the contract should have enough eth to cover both

**File(s) affected:**`ReachAffiliateDistribution.sol`

**Description:** In `ReachAffiliateDistribution.swapEth()`, part of the `_ethAmount` is transferred to the main distribution contract. Specifically, `uint256 commissionAmount = (_ethAmount * commission) / 100;` is transferred to the `mainDistribution` contract. However, in the lines that follow, it swaps `_ethAmount` of ETH for `REACH` tokens, without accounting for the `commissionAmount` that was already transferred out, which can lead to serious accounting issues because the contract might not have enough ETH for the swap.

```
router.swapExactETHForTokensSupportingFeeOnTransferTokens{
    value: _ethAmount
}(_outputAmount, path, address(this), block.timestamp + 5);
```

Furthermore, if the extra ETH amount that is being swapped is part of the user’s distribution, this could block some users from claiming their rewards.

**Recommendation:** In the swap function, change `value` to `_ethAmount - commissionAmount` to properly account for the amount transferred out.
