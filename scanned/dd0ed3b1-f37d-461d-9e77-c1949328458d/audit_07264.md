# Quota Manager Can Cause Recalled Funds to Be Stuck - Phase 3

## Summary
The report discusses a bug in the ETH and ERC-20 token transfer process through the `ERC20Vault` and `Bridge` contracts. When sending funds from these contracts to users, the `consumeQuota` function is called on the `QuotaManager` contract. However, when sending funds from users back to the contracts, the quota is not checked. This can result in a user being unable to claim their funds back on the source chain if the amount exceeds the maximum quota. The Taiko team has acknowledged the issue but has not made any changes, stating that it is unlikely to occur with most assets. It is recommended to monitor assets with a quota to avoid this scenario.

## Details
ETH and ERC-20 tokens can be sent through the [`ERC20Vault`](https://github.com/taikoxyz/taiko-mono/blob/dd8725f8d27f835102fa3c5a013003090268357d/packages/protocol/contracts/tokenvault/ERC20Vault.sol#L223) and [`Bridge`](https://github.com/taikoxyz/taiko-mono/blob/dd8725f8d27f835102fa3c5a013003090268357d/packages/protocol/contracts/bridge/Bridge.sol#L126) contracts, respectively. When funds are sent from these contracts to the users, [`consumeQuota`](https://github.com/taikoxyz/taiko-mono/blob/dd8725f8d27f835102fa3c5a013003090268357d/packages/protocol/contracts/bridge/QuotaManager.sol#L60) is called on the `QuotaManager` contract.


However, the quota is not checked when sending funds from the users to the contracts. This makes it possible for a user to send funds, mark the message as failed on the destination chain, but not be able to recall their message and claim their funds back on the source chain [[1]](https://github.com/taikoxyz/taiko-mono/blob/dd8725f8d27f835102fa3c5a013003090268357d/packages/protocol/contracts/bridge/Bridge.sol#L190) [[2]](https://github.com/taikoxyz/taiko-mono/blob/dd8725f8d27f835102fa3c5a013003090268357d/packages/protocol/contracts/tokenvault/ERC20Vault.sol#L347) if the amount exceeds the maximum quota. Hence, these funds would be stuck until the quota is increased or removed.


Consider validating during message sending that the funds sent are lower than the current quota to avoid issues when recalling messages.


***Update:** Acknowledged, not resolved. The likelihood of this issue justifies not introducing additional code changes. However, assets that have a quota are recommended to be monitored to check for the scenario depicted above. The Taiko team stated:*



> *Quota only applies to tje ERC20 vault and we believe for ETH and tokens whose quota is configured to be non-zero (USDC, USDT, TKO), it is very unlikely the claiming will fail thus the message be marked as failed on the destination chain.*
