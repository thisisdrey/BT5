# [M-02] Diamond upgrade proposition can be falsified

## Summary
This bug report is about a vulnerability found in the DiamondCutFacet.sol, LibDiamond.sol and LibDiamond.sol contracts. It states that the id of the proposal is determined by only its facet changes, which are hashed, skipping two critical pieces of data - the `_init` and `_calldata`. This means that during a diamond upgrade, developers can choose what code will be executed by the contract using a delegatecall and make the contract perform any actions of their choice. 

The recommended mitigation steps for this vulnerability are to add `_init` and `_calldata` to the proposition hash. This vulnerability was confirmed and resolved by connext/nxtp@63badc8 and the severity was decreased to `medium` by 0xleastwood.

Diamond is to be upgraded after a certain delay to give time to the community to verify changes made by the developers. If the proposition can be falsified, the contract admins can exploit the contract in any way of their choice.

### Proof of Concept

To determine the id of the proposal, only its facet changes are hashed, skipping two critical pieces of data - the `_init` and `_calldata`. During a diamond upgrade, devs can choose what code will be executed **by the contract using a delegatecall**. Thus, they can make the contract perform **any** actions of their choice.

### Recommended Mitigation Steps

Add `_init` and `_calldata` to the proposition hash.
