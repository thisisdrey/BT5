# Accounting discrepancy in rollover bids

## Summary
This bug report is about a discrepancy between the balances of lockedCollateralLedger and encumberedCollateralBalances, which could lead to accounting errors and unexpected reverts. When a rollover bid is transferred to a new TermRepoCollateralManager, TermAuction calls TermRepoCollateralManager.acceptRolloverCollateral() after transferring the collateral tokens. However, acceptRolloverCollateral() only modifies the value of lockedCollateralLedger[borrower][collateralToken] but does not increase encumberedCollateralBalances[collateralToken] by the same amount as it occurs in _lockCollateral. This could lead to an accounting error that could lead to reverts when _unlockCollateral() is called for the borrower and encumberedCollateralBalances[collateralToken] could be reduced below zero.

The recommendation for this bug was to review the logic of TermRepoCollateralManager.acceptRolloverCollateral() and to modify the balances of encumberedCollateralBalances[collateralToken] by the same amount as lockedCollateralLedger[borrower][collateralToken]. After a retest of commit e883f0e, the issue was resolved as acceptRolloverCollateral now also increments balances in encumberedCollateralBalances ledger.

## Details
## Description
Discrepancy between balances of `lockedCollateralLedger` and `encumberedCollateralBalances` could lead to accounting errors and unexpected reverts. When a rollover bid is transferred to a new `TermRepoCollateralManager`, `TermAuction` calls `TermRepoCollateralManager.acceptRolloverCollateral()` after transferring the collateral tokens. However, `acceptRolloverCollateral()` only modifies the value of `lockedCollateralLedger[borrower][collateralToken]`. 

It does not increase `encumberedCollateralBalances[collateralToken]` by the same amount, as it occurs in `_lockCollateral`. This could lead to an accounting error that could lead to reverts when `_unlockCollateral()` is called for the borrower and `encumberedCollateralBalances[collateralToken]` could be reduced below zero.

## Recommendations
- Review the logic of `TermRepoCollateralManager.acceptRolloverCollateral()`.
- Modify balances of `encumberedCollateralBalances[collateralToken]` by the same amount as `lockedCollateralLedger[borrower][collateralToken]`.

## Resolution
Based on a retest of commit `e883f0e`, the issue has been resolved - `acceptRolloverCollateral` now also increments balances in the `encumberedCollateralBalances` ledger.
