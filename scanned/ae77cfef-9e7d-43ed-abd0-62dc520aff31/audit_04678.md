# (HAL-03) MAIL SERVER MISCONFIGURATION

## Summary
The `bitsCrunch` mail server had a problem that allowed someone to pretend to send emails on behalf of `bitsCrunch` and potentially scam or defraud users. This was proven when a fake email was sent from `noreply@bitscrunch.com` to a victim's email, `pau.carrasco@halborn.com`. The victim was then directed to a malicious website. The impact of this bug is rated as a 4 out of 5, and the likelihood is also a 4 out of 5. To fix this issue, the `SPF` and `DMARC` records need to be configured with the correct policies. This has been solved by setting the SPF to `v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all` and the DMARC to `v=DMARC1; p= quarantine; rua=mailto:dd62a1cbfae64407adc2335298e1df48@dmarc-reports.cloudflare.net`.

## Details
##### Description

`bitsCrunch` mail server contained multiple misconfigurations, allowing an attacker to impersonate emails as `bitsCrunch` and performing scams/fraud/phishing users, which may result in financial and customer loss.

##### Proof of Concept

* An email was sent under false pretences, purporting to originate from the email address `noreply@bitscrunch.com`.

##### Score

Impact: 4

##### Recommendation

Configure the `SPF` and `DMARC` records with the appropriate policies, preventing an attacker from spoofing emails on behalf of the company.

##### Remediation

**SOLVED**: The issue was solved by configuring the SPF `v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all` and the DMARK to `v=DMARC1; p= quarantine; rua=mailto:dd62a1cbfae64407adc2335298e1df48@dmarc-reports.cloudflare.net`
