# Users May Force One Another Out of Tiers

## Summary
A bug has been reported in the `SPTV2.sol` file. The bug allows users to purchase subscriptions for another user and block them from accessing a higher tier subscription. This can be problematic when there is a highly exclusive tier with more benefits. The bug has been marked as "Fixed" by the client and the code has been updated to address the issue. The recommendation is to ensure that only the caller or a signature from the recipient address can mint advanced subscriptions for someone else.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Fixed" by the client. Addressed in: `cb5470d2ca3af7de27a2beea2d6a0978740f386b`.

If a subscription is being purchased for an account other than the calling address, the subscription tier can no longer be changed.

**File(s) affected:**`SPTV2.sol`

**Description:** This vulnerability was brought to our attention by the Subscription Token Team. `STPV2.mintAdvanced()` allows callers to specify both the recipient and the tier when purchasing a subscription. This creates a situation in which users can purchases subscriptions for another user with the intent of blocking them out of a favorable subscription tier. This becomes problematic in the situation where there is a highly exclusive subscription tier. The potential reward benefits from joining that tier could be more profitable than it would be to purchase subscriptions of a lower tier for an address that one may suspect to try to join the higher tier.

**Recommendation:** When minting advanced for a recipient, ensure that either the recipient is the caller, or, if the team wishes to support advanced minting for addresses that are not limited to just the caller, consider incorporating a signature that can be checked from the receiving address.
