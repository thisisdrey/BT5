# Unclear Mechanism of Detecting and Handling Costfree Wallets

## Summary
A recent update has addressed a bug in the `BWGToken.sol` file. The team has updated the documentation to clarify that cost-free wallets will only be used for internal wallets of the company, to prevent unnecessary transaction fees. However, it is still advised to make this information even clearer in the end-user documentation. The bug was related to the selection of cost-free wallets, which was not clearly documented in the contract or whitepaper. This could potentially be exploited by malicious users to avoid paying storage fees. The recommendation is to document the criteria for selecting cost-free wallets, taking into account cases where users may transfer tokens to others to avoid fees.

## Details
**Update**
Addressed in `86dc611b71111c038fbf0f1c30ed15f6d2fd2a17` and in provided draft documentation. The team has updated the documentation, stating that _"the cost-free wallets will only be used for internal wallets of the BrettonWoods digital AG, to prevent storage cost deductions from internal wallets and thus prevent unnecessary transaction fees"_. We would still advise making it even more clear in end-user documentation.

**File(s) affected:**`BWGToken.sol`

**Description:** Though the concept of a costable wallet and a cost-free wallet is clear, how to determine them is not clearly documented in the contract or whitepaper. In `beforeTokenTransfer()` function of `BWGToken.sol`, it can be noticed that a token receiver is automatically appended into the `costableList`. However, it is not clear how cost-free wallets are selected. Since cost-free wallets are exempted from storage costs, malicious users may abuse this to avoid cost payment.

**Exploit Scenario:** Assume that a wallet address is in the `costFreeList`. He may disclose the information, and help other users to store their tokens. Such mechanisms may also cause fraud and other unintended behaviors.

**Recommendation:** It is recommended to document the `costFreeWallet` selection criteria. Such criteria should consider the potential cases where users may transfer tokens to others in order to avoid storage fees.
