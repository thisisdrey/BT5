# Unverified Provider Reports

## Summary
The report discusses a vulnerability in RelayPayment, where the function updateProviderPaymentStorageWithComplainerCU is called with relay.UnresponsiveProviders as an argument. This function updates the provider payment storage with information from consumers who reported unresponsive providers. However, there is no specific verification step to confirm that the reported unresponsive providers are actually associated with the reporting consumer. This could result in unjust penalties being imposed on providers for issues they may not be accountable for. To fix this, a validation step should be integrated before updating the provider payment storage. This issue has been fixed in P.R. #1225.

## Details
## Vulnerability Report

In `RelayPayment`, there is a call to `updateProviderPaymentStorageWithComplainerCU`, passing `relay.UnresponsiveProviders` as an argument. This function updates the provider payment storage with Consumer-Unit (CU) information from consumers who reported unresponsive providers.

```go
func (k msgServer) RelayPayment(goCtx context.Context, msg *types.MsgRelayPayment) (*types.MsgRelayPaymentResponse, error) {
    [...]
    // update provider payment storage with complainer's CU
    err = k.updateProviderPaymentStorageWithComplainerCU(ctx, relay.UnresponsiveProviders, logger, epochStart, relay.SpecId, relay.CuSum, servicersToPair, project.Index)
    [...]
}
```

The vulnerability arises due to the lack of a specific verification step to confirm whether the unresponsive providers reported by consumers (`relay.UnresponsiveProviders`) are genuinely associated with the reporting consumer. Consumers may inaccurately report providers with whom they are not genuinely paired. As a result, executing `updateProviderPaymentStorageWithComplainerCU` may adjust the provider payment storage based on unreliable information, resulting in unjust penalties imposed on providers for issues they may not be accountable for.

## Remediation

Integrate a validation step to precede the update of the provider payment storage, ensuring that the consumer reporting unresponsive providers is genuinely paired with the providers being reported.

## Patch

Fixed in PR #1225.
