# No Restriction on Maker and Taker Fees

## Summary
The client has reported a bug in the `BountyV1.sol` file, where the `setMakerFee()` and `setTakerFee()` functions do not have an upper limit on the fees that can be charged. This could potentially lead to an exploit. The client has recommended enforcing an upper limit on the fees to prevent this issue. The bug has been fixed and marked as resolved in a recent update.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Fixed" by the client. Addressed in: `4fd63caeba91309bbeed633752a362591bc2bf2c`. The client provided the following explanation:

> Set max-fee to 100% for both maker and taker. In practice we won't ever get near those numbers, but allowing a higher-than-100% fee could be an exploit. Also, it's okay (technically speaking) for maker + taker to exceed 100%, since each is assessed as a fraction in sequence. So we could take 60% of 100, and then 50% of 40, for a total of 80 in fees. (But, again, we'll never realistically be close to that.)

**File(s) affected:**`BountyV1.sol`

**Description:** The functions `setMakerFee()` and `setTakerFee()` do not have an upper bound on the fees. The fees could exceed 100%.

**Recommendation:** Enforce an upper bound on the protocol fees.
