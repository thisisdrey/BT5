# Traders Can Prevent LPs From Removing Liquidity Through Denial of Service

## Summary
A bug has been found in the `Pool.sol` file where the amount of funds available for withdrawal is affected by the `guaranteedValue` of borrowed tokens. This means that if traders collectively borrow a large amount of tokens, it can significantly reduce the funds that can be withdrawn from the protocol. It is recommended to impose a global limit on long position sizes to prevent this issue.

## Details
**Update**
Fixed. Global long position sizes can now be limited across the range of tokens. However, it is up to the client to correctly set these limits. The client's response: "We added limit for both long and short positions. These changes is deployed."

**File(s) affected:**`Pool.sol`

**Description:** When calling `removeLiquidity()`, the amount of funds to which an LP is entitled is calculated with reference to the `poolValue` within a tranche, i.e. the `poolAmount` in dollars minus the `reservedAmount` in dollars plus the `guaranteedValue`. The `guaranteedValue` is the total amount borrowed (in USD) by traders. However, when removing liquidity, the only tokens that may be taken out are those counted by `poolAmount`; that is, when removing liquidity, `poolAmount` is decreased until it reaches zero and it cannot go lower. Thus, if traders collectively borrow enough tokens from the protocol, they may dramatically reduce the amount of funds that can be withdrawn from the protocol at a given time.

This may even occur for long periods of time without malicious intent, say, in the event that some traders lose their keys and their trades move in their favour.

**Recommendation:** Impose a global limit on long position sizes to ensure a sizeable fraction of LPs is always able to withdraw funds.
