# The voting rules are exploitable for one to do self-challenge at a very low cost hence pre- venting real challenges from others 

## Summary
This bug report discusses an issue in the code for a voting system. The code allows for a user, named Alice, to protect her funds by creating a fake vote against herself using her other addresses. This allows her to control the outcome of the vote and lock her funds, making them safe from real challenges. The report recommends setting a minimum threshold for a certain variable to prevent this exploitation. The developer has acknowledged the issue and will fix it.

## Details
## Context

**File:** Voting.sol#L163

## Description

Suppose Alice is a pool owner and she wants to protect her funds from being challenged by other players. To do this, she can use one of her other addresses to create a challenge vote against herself. In this scenario, both the defender address and the attacker address belong to Alice.

Alice can select an extremely low `voterPercent`, making the amount of money she may lose through this practice negligible. Since both parties in the vote are hers, she does not mind which one wins; however, it is reasonable that she would try to ensure the defender side wins, as that is her main account. 

Because the `voterPercent` is so low, other players are less incentivized to participate in the vote, resulting in Alice primarily controlling the outcome. This allows her to lock her funds in this particular vote, effectively shielding them from real challenges posed by other players.

Once this vote concludes, Alice can initiate a new vote as the placeholder again. This mechanism allows her to continuously exploit the rules to protect her funds from genuine challenges at a negligible cost. 

It is important to note that Alice should not set `voterPercent` to exactly 0, as that would prevent the vote from being finalized, leading to her funds being locked indefinitely.

## Recommendation

Set a reasonable minimum threshold for `voterPercent` and do not allow it to be extremely low or close to zero.
