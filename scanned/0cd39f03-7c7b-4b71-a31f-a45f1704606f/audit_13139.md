# Lockup Plans Are Not Well Suited for Trading on Traditional OTC Platforms

## Summary
This bug report is about a vulnerability in Over-The-Counter (OTC) trading platforms that use a Request For Quote (RFQ) style. When a trade participant requests to buy a specific plan ID and gives a fixed amount of time to fill that order, the taker of such an order can take advantage of the situation by batching two transactions in one block. This involves segmenting the plan ID into two, with one having only 1 wei to vest and the other with the rest. The large plan will have an incremented plan ID and the small plan will have the old ID, allowing the taker to fill the order and get the full payment for what is now a worthless plan token. 

To prevent this vulnerability, it is recommended that both plans be assigned a new ID during the segmentation process. This way, the vulnerability can be prevented and trade participants can be sure that their orders are safe.

## Details
#### Description


For most of the OTC trading platforms with RFQ style the maker or the taker creates an order that is valid for some time and is expecting a specific token ID. In case of a lockup period a trade participants can request to buy a specific plan ID and then give a fixed amount of time to fill that order, assuming that anything past that time that is unvested is guaranteed to go to them. In reality, the taker of such an order can batch two transactions in one block:


1. Segment the `planId` the order is expecting into two: one with just 1 wei to vest and the other with the rest. The large plan will have an incremented plan ID. The small plan will have the old ID.
2. Fill the order and get the full payment for what is now a worthless plan token.


People should be aware of such a possibility before attempting to purchase any lockup plans over OTC platforms.


#### Recommendation


One way to solve this is to assign both plans a new ID during the segmentation process.
