# Users Might Expect Non-Existent Rewards

## Summary
The client has acknowledged a bug where pending rewards are visible to users, but there may not be enough ETH or REACH in the contract to cover them. This affects the `ReachAffiliateDistribution.sol` and `ReachMainDistribution.sol` files, specifically the `createDistribution()` function. The function takes in three inputs, but if the total amount to be distributed is not equal to the total amount in the Merkle tree or exceeds the current balance of the contract, some users may not receive their expected rewards. It is recommended to clarify this issue in user-facing documentation, as there is no easy solution.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Acknowledged" by the client. The client provided the following explanation:

> Pending rewards are visible to users and we fill the contract with some reach and ETH to cover for any margin of error

Note that, in this case, the owner has the responsibility of making sure there is enough `ETH` or `REACH` in the contract.

**File(s) affected:**`ReachAffiliateDistribution.sol`, `ReachMainDistribution.sol`

**Description:** The function `createDistribution()` accepts three input arguments: the `_merkleRoot` containing all the users and the amount (both ETH and REACH) each user should get, and the `_ethAmount` and `_reachAmount` inputs representing the total amount to be distributed in both tokens, and should be equal to the total amount to be distributed to all the leaves in the Merkle tree, and also less or equal to the current ETH or token balance of the contract. Otherwise, some users would be expecting rewards that are not even available.

**Recommendation:** The Merkle root is used for efficiency to reduce gas consumption. However, it also makes it hard to validate input data making the contract more centralized. There is no easy solution for this problem and we would recommend making this clear in user-facing documentation.
