# [M-01] use of transfer() instead of call()  to send eth

## Summary
This bug report is related to the Ethereum blockchain. After the Istanbul hardfork, there has been an increase in the gas cost of the SLOAD operation which has broken some existing smart contracts. These contracts have broken because their fallback functions used to consume less than 2300 gas, and they’ll now consume more than that. Any smart contract that uses `transfer()` or `send()` is taking a hard dependency on gas costs by forwarding a fixed amount of gas, i.e. 2300. This might render ETH impossible to withdraw.

Proof of concept were provided in the report. The tools used for manual review were also mentioned. The recommended mitigation step for this bug is to use `call()` to send ETH instead of `transfer()` or `send()`.

Two comments were added to the report by maxsam4 (Sushi Miso) and ghoul-sol (judge). Maxsam4 stated that this is intentional, not a risk and that the contract does not want to give any gas stipend to the destination. Ghoul-sol commented that using `.transfer` can make ETH transfer to a smart contract impossible and agreed with the warden that this is an issue.

#### Impact

Use of `transfer()` might render ETH impossible to withdraw because after istanbul hardfork, there is an increase in the gas cost of the SLOAD operation and therefore breaks some existing smart contracts.Those contracts will break because their fallback functions used to consume less than 2300 gas, and they’ll now consume more, since 2300 the amount of gas a contract’s fallback function receives if it’s called via Solidity’s `transfer()` or `send()` methods.
Any smart contract that uses `transfer()` or `send()` is taking a hard dependency on gas costs by forwarding a fixed amount of gas: 2300.
