# `voteDelegate` can perform any arbitrary calls on behalf of `VoterProxy`

## Summary
A bug was discovered in the `VoterProxy` contract defined at https://github.com/convex-eth/platform/blob/754d9e700693246275b613e895b4044b63ce9ed5/contracts/contracts/VoterProxy.sol#L130. The `vote` function in the contract can be called by the `voteDelegate` through the `Booster` contract and can call any arbitrary contract on behalf of `VoterProxy`. This poses a high risk to users' money as `VoterProxy` is the main contract that holds it.

To address this issue, it is recommended that a whitelist of `_votingAddress` be implemented. This whitelist would ensure that only approved contracts are called by the `VoterProxy` contract, thus reducing the risk of users' money being compromised.

## Details
##### Description
Function `vote` in `VoterProxy` defined at https://github.com/convex-eth/platform/blob/754d9e700693246275b613e895b4044b63ce9ed5/contracts/contracts/VoterProxy.sol#L130 accepts call from `voteDelegate` through `Booster` contract and can call any arbitrary contract on behalf of `VoterProxy`. Since `VoterProxy` is main contract that holds users money it's highly risked to allow arbitrary contracts calls.

##### Recommendation
We strictly recommend to whitelist `_votingAddress`
