# Merkle tree and proof depth not checked 

## Summary
This bug report is about a potential vulnerability in the SablierV2MerkleLL and SablierV2MerkleLockup smart contracts. The issue is that the length of the Merkle proof is not being checked against the expected depth of the Merkle tree. This means that a malicious team member could potentially exploit the system by making one of the leaves of the Merkle tree a root hash of another tree, which could hold their own list of addresses and amounts. This could be prevented by storing the tree depth and validating the proof length during the claim process. However, the Sablier team has decided to leave this as it is and suggests that allowing clawback during the grace period could help to lower the risk of exploits for users. The Cantina Managed team has acknowledged this issue.

## Context
- `SablierV2MerkleLL.sol#L59-L96`

## Description
When claiming an airstream, the Merkle proof is checked against the Merkle root, but the length of the proof is not checked against the expected depth of the Merkle tree.

```solidity
function _checkClaim(uint256 index, bytes32 leaf, bytes32[] calldata merkleProof) internal view {
    if (_claimedBitMap.get(index)) {
        revert Errors.SablierV2MerkleLockup_StreamClaimed(index);
    }
    if (!MerkleProof.verify(merkleProof, MERKLE_ROOT, leaf)) {
        revert Errors.SablierV2MerkleLockup_InvalidProof();
    }
}
```

The way this can be exploited is if a malicious member of the team deploying the `MerkleLockup` makes one of the leaves of the Merkle tree a root hash of another tree (which can hold their own list of addresses, amounts, and indexes even outside of the expected index range). This would, however, be visible to anyone that actually validates the Merkle tree of course, as one of the leaves is not the hash of the address, amount, and index; it would just be the precomputed root hash of a subtree.

## Recommendation
When creating the `MerkleLockup`, consider also storing the tree depth and validating the proof length against it during the claim process.

## Additional Notes from Teams
**Sablier:** Having a tree inside another tree presents a very interesting edge case. We also acknowledge that this could be a valid problem. However, we would like to leave this as it is. If there is a team member with malicious intent, he could exploit the campaign creator in several ways, such as introducing new leaves with malicious amounts assigned to themselves. We also believe that allowing clawback during the grace period will lower the risk of exploits to the users.

**Cantina Managed:** Acknowledged.
