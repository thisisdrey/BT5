# Untracked Transfer Recipient

## Summary
The bug report discusses an issue with the behavior of sbt_soul_transfer, a function used in a program called sbt_soul_transfer. The problem occurs when the transfer process is resumed and a different recipient is specified. The program does not keep track of the recipient, which can result in Soulbound Tokens being split into multiple accounts. This can also lead to other issues, such as transferring tokens to banned accounts and unexpected distribution of tokens. The report provides a proof of concept and recommends a fix to keep track of recipients in the program's ongoing_soul_tx feature. The NDC team has acknowledged the issue and suggests disabling ongoing_soul_tx or implementing the recommended fix to prevent future problems.

## Details
## Vulnerability Analysis: sbt_soul_transfer Behavior

The issue pertains to `sbt_soul_transfer`’s behavior during the resumption of an ongoing Soulbound Token transfer process when a different recipient is specified. 

`sbt_soul_transfer` calls `transfer_continuation`, which checks if there is an ongoing Soulbound Token transfer associated with the `from` account by looking up the `from` account in the `ongoing_soul_tx` mapping. If such a transfer is associated with the `from` account, it resumes the transfer process.

However, `ongoing_soul_tx` does not keep track of the transfer recipient. Thus, the caller may change the recipient in each `sbt_soul_transfer` call, splitting Soulbound Tokens into several accounts.

Aside from splitting Soulbound Tokens, how `ongoing_soul_tx` is handled has several other implications. For instance, transfer recipients are only validated in `start_transfer_with_continuation` on transfer initiation when `ongoing_soul_tx.get(from)` is `None`. It is possible to bypass such checks and transfer Soulbound Tokens to banned accounts by changing recipients on follow-up calls to `sbt_soul_transfer`.

Another example is that `sbt_soul_transfer` and `sbt_recover` both utilize `ongoing_soul_tx` to store the resume point of ongoing Soulbound Token movements. Due to sharing the same checkpoint, if calls to those two functions interleave, Soulbound Tokens may be distributed unexpectedly to several accounts.

## Proof of Concept

Here we outline how to split Soulbound Tokens into two accounts.

1. **Initial Soulbound Token Transfer**:
    - Account A initiates a Soulbound Token transfer to recipient B utilizing `sbt_soul_transfer`.

2. **Check for Completion**:
    - The amount of Soulbound Tokens owned by Account A exceeds the max transfer limit; thus, it stores a transfer checkpoint for A to the `ongoing_soul_tx` mapping for subsequent processing.

3. **Resuming Soulbound Token Transfer**:
    - A resumes the transfer process by calling `_sbt_soul_transfer` again, this time supplying a different recipient, account C.

4. **Transfer Execution**:
    - As there are no checks on whether the recipient between two calls to `sbt_soul_transfer` match, the second call successfully transfers Soulbound Tokens to C, resulting in token splits.

## Remediation

Keep track of recipients in `ongoing_soul_tx`, and utilize those stored recipients on subsequent calls to `sbt_soul_transfer` and `sbt_recover`.

## Patch

The NDC team acknowledges this finding but also states that any account’s maximum amount of Soulbound Tokens would not exceed the maximum transfer limit under the current environment. This implies `ongoing_soul_tx` is currently unused, and the abovementioned issues will not occur. However, to prevent future issues when the amount of ownable Soulbound Tokens grows, we still recommend disabling `ongoing_soul_tx` altogether or applying the fix recommended above.
