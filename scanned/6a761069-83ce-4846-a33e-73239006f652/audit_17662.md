# [H-12] In `ERC20`, `TotalSupply` is broken

## Summary
This bug report is about an issue with the ERC20 contract found in the Lending Market repository. The issue is that the `_totalSupply` is not being initialized to 0, which can lead to an inaccurate total supply and cause issues with integrations, computations of market cap, etc. 

To demonstrate the issue, the bug reporter deployed the ERC20 with `totalSupply_ = 1000`, which resulted in the `totalSupply` function returning 1000, which is incorrect. They then minted 1000 tokens, which resulted in 1000 tokens in the market, but due to `_totalSupply += amount;`, the totalSupply was 2000, which is still incorrect.

The recommended mitigation step is to remove `_initialSupply`. The judge also recommended that the bug reporter err on the side of giving too much information to avoid getting their finding invalidated incorrectly. After further thinking, the judge still believes the finding is of high severity as the ERC20 standard is also broken.

For an obscure reason as it’s not commented, `_totalSupply` is not initialized to 0, leading to an inaccurate total supply, which could easily break integrations, computations of market cap, etc.

### Proof of Concept

If the constructor is called with `_initialSupply = 1000`, then `1000` tokens are minted. The total supply will be `2000`.

### Recommended Mitigation Steps

Remove `_initialSupply`.
