# Errors in the white paper’s equation for index calculation

## Summary
This bug report is about an incorrect equation in the Fuji Protocol white paper. The equation used to calculate the index for a given token at a specific time does not accurately reflect the protocol's implementation. This means that users may lose funds if they rely on the white paper's information when using the protocol. The report recommends replacing the equation with a correct and simplified version in the short term and ensuring that all equations in the white paper are accurate and in line with the protocol's implementation in the long term.

## Details
## Difficulty: Undetermined

## Type: Auditing and Logging

## Description
The white paper uses the following equation (equation 1) to describe how the index for a given token at timestamp \( t \) is calculated:

\[
I_t = I_{t-1} + \left( \frac{B_{t-1}}{B} \right) - B_t
\]

Where \( B \) is the amount of the given token that the Fuji Protocol owes the provider (the borrowing protocol) at timestamp \( t \).

The index is updated only when the balance changes through the accrual of interest, not when the balance changes through borrowing or repayment operations. This means that \( B_t - B_{t-1} \) is always negative, which is incorrect, as \( r \) should calculate the interest rate since the last index update.

* \( r_3 \)
* \( r_2 \)
* ...
* \( r_n \)

A user's current balance is computed by taking the user’s initial stored balance, multiplying it by the current index, and dividing it by the index at the time of the creation of that user's position. The division operation ensures that the user will not owe interest that accrued before the creation of the user’s position. The index provides an efficient way to keep track of interest rates without having to update each user's balance separately, which would be prohibitively expensive on Ethereum.

However, interest is compounded through multiplication, not addition. The formula should use the product sign instead of the plus sign.

## Exploit Scenario
Alice decides to use the Fuji Protocol after reading the white paper. She later learns that calculations in the white paper do not match the implementations in the protocol. Because Alice allocated her funds based on her understanding of the specification, she loses funds.

## Recommendations
**Short term:** Replace equation 1 in the white paper with a correct and simplified version. For more information on the simplified version, see finding TOB-FUJI-015.

\[
I_t = \frac{I_{t-1}}{B} \cdot B_t
\]

**Long term:** Ensure that the equations in the white paper are correct and in sync with the implementation.
