# Pool owner can sandwich deposits into his pool to keep their fs at 1 while being able to withdraw ETH earnings 

## Summary
The report discusses a bug in the trust score system for a pool that depends on three factors: total ETH staked, Booster, and financial stability. The financial stability is calculated based on the ratio of current ETH earnings to maximum ETH earnings. However, pool owners can bypass the penalty for withdrawing ETH earnings by monitoring the mempool and withdrawing the same amount they will receive in owner fees from upcoming ETH staking transactions. This loophole allows pool owners to withdraw liquid ETH without affecting their financial stability. The likelihood of this exploit being used by many pool owners is high. The report suggests introducing more incentives to encourage pool owners to keep their ETH earnings high. The report concludes that the bug will be fixed.

**Description:**  
The trust score depends on three factors: total ETH staked in the pool, Booster, and financial stability. Financial stability is the ratio of current ETH earnings to maximum ETH earnings. The reason pool owners might not claim their ETH earnings is to avoid decreasing their financial stability. 

The purpose of the financial stability (FS) formula is to impose a temporary penalty on pool owners for withdrawing ETH earnings; however, this penalty can be bypassed. A pool owner can monitor the mempool for `ethStake` transactions into their pool and withdraw as much ETH as they are going to receive in owner fees from the upcoming `ethStake`.

**Likelihood:**  
Many of the pool owners could be interested in this exploit, as it would allow them to withdraw liquid ETH for any purpose without affecting their financial stability.

## Proof of Concept:
1. Bob signs an `ethStake` transaction that is going to stake 50 ETH into Alice's pool.
2. Alice frontruns Bob and withdraws 1 ETH from her ETH earnings, decreasing her financial stability.
3. Bob's transaction makes Alice 1 ETH in owner fees, so Alice's FS is instantly restored to 1.
