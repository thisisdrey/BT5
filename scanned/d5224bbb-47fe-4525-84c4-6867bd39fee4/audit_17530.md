# [H-01] Total supply can be incorrect in `ERC20`

## Summary
Bug report submitted by Picodes on the lending-market-v2 repository. The issue is that the `_totalSupply` can be initialized to something other than 0, which could lead to an inaccurate total supply and break integrations, computations of market cap, etc. For example, if the constructor is called with `_initialSupply = 1000`, the `totalSupply` will be initialized to 1000, leading to a discrepancy of 1000 between the real total supply and the one of the contract.

The recommended mitigation steps are to remove `_initialSupply`. This was acknowledged by nivasan1 (Canto) and commented on by Alex the Entreprenerd (judge). Alex the Entreprenerd (judge) then commented that the issue had been mitigated in the V2 code in scope and recommended changing the code to use `_mint` as it the code in scope will not emit an event. Alex the Entreprenerd (judge) admitted to making a mistake here.

## Details
_Submitted by Picodes_

<https://github.com/Plex-Engineer/lending-market-v2/blob/ea5840de72eab58bec837bb51986ac73712fcfde/contracts/ERC20.sol#L33>
<https://github.com/Plex-Engineer/lending-market/blob/ab31a612be354e252d72faead63d86b844172761/contracts/ERC20.sol#L95>

`_totalSupply` can be initialized to something different than 0, which would lead to an inaccurate total supply, and could easily break integrations, computations of market cap, etc.

### Proof of Concept

If the constructor is called with \_initialSupply = 1000, the `totalSupply` will be initialized to 1000.

As all the others computations are correct, there will be for ever a discrepancy of 1000 between the real total supply and the one of the contract.

### Recommended Mitigation Steps

Remove `_initialSupply`.
