# payFastLane Could Be Continuously Blocked by Exploiting selfdestruct

## Summary
A bug has been identified in the UpdateManager contract that could allow a malicious contract to block calls to the payFastLane function and potentially exploit ETH. This is due to a strict equality check on the contract's balance and the msg.value, which can only return to zero after a successful call to update. This means that an attacker could repeatedly front-run legitimate calls to update and block the payFastLane function. It is recommended to implement a different mechanism to determine if the fast lane is being used to prevent this potential exploit.

## Details
A malicious contract could exploit the `selfdestruct` opcode to send ETH directly to the `UpdateManager` contract, blocking calls to the [`payFastLane` function](https://github.com/Fantom-foundation/Bridge/blob/558465d3aba2ffae1a4436a2fc14c723b82926df/contracts/UpdateManager.sol#L61) until the next call to [`update`](https://github.com/Fantom-foundation/Bridge/blob/558465d3aba2ffae1a4436a2fc14c723b82926df/contracts/UpdateManager.sol#L36). This occurs due to the strict equality check of the contract's balance having to be equal to the `msg.value`. Since the contract balance only returns to zero after a successful call to `update`, an attacker could even front\-run legitimate calls to update and recover their ETH, and keep repeating the process to block the `payFastLane` functionality.


Consider implementing an alternative mechanism to determine whether the fast lane is in use.
