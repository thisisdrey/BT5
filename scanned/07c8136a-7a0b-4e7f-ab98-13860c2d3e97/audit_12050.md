# Mechanism to Prevent Owner From Bidding in Their Own Auction Can Be Easily Bypassed

## Summary
This bug report addresses an issue where users are able to bid on their own auctions and make offers on items they own in the NFTMarketAuction and NFTMarketOffers functions. This bypasses the intended mechanism and creates a false sense of supply and demand. The client has marked this bug as "Mitigated" and has added checks to prevent token owners from making/accepting offers on their own NFT. However, the report recommends not relying on this mechanism due to the Sybil resistance problem.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Mitigated" by the client. Addressed in: `714cdb08c6616c64ca26c30b3c10d6ac37054b70`. The client provided the following explanation:

> As noted, we have added checks to NFTMarketOffers.offerOnNft() and NFTMarketOffers.acceptOffer() to prevent token owners from making/accepting offers on their own NFT. And, as you mentioned, there is currently no effective way to overcome the Sybil resistance problem, but we cannot eliminate it because its purpose is to reduce the likelihood of human error.

**File(s) affected:**`NFTMarketAuction`, `NFTMarketOffers`

**Description:** The `NFTMarketAuction.bid()` function contains the following line:

```
require(
      msg.sender != marketItem.ownerAddress,
      "Auction owner can't place bid on his Auction"
    );
```

However, it is very easy for the owner to bid on their own auction with a different address, effectively bypassing this mechanism.

Furthermore, the function `NFTMarketOffers.offerOnNft()` allows a user to place offers on items they own. Combined with `acceptOffer()`, which allows users to accept their own offers, this creates an illusion of supply and demand for a given item.

**Recommendation:** There is no good way to overcome the Sybil resistance problem currently, so ensure that the protocol does not rely on this mechanism being satisfied.
