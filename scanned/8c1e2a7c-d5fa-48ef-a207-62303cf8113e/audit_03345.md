# [M-05] Price Granularity Limited by Payment Token Decimals: Cannot List Tokens Cheaper than 0.000001 USDT

## Summary
The bug report is about a limitation in the SecondSwap marketplace where tokens cannot be listed for less than 0.000001 USDT or its equivalent in other 6 decimal payment tokens. This is due to the fact that the marketplace enforces a minimum price floor based on the payment token's smallest unit. This limitation can prevent the listing and trading of low-value tokens and may have a significant impact, especially for memecoins. The root cause of this issue is that the `pricePerUnit` must be greater than 0 and represents the price of 1 vesting token in the payment token's smallest unit. The team at SecondSwap has acknowledged this issue and is working on a solution.

## Details
<https://github.com/code-423n4/2024-12-secondswap/blob/214849c3517eb26b31fe194bceae65cb0f52d2c0/contracts/SecondSwap_Marketplace.sol# L256>

The SecondSwap marketplace enforces a minimum price floor based on the payment token’s smallest unit which will commonly be USDT. This creates a limitation where tokens cannot be listed for less than 0.000001 USDT (or equivalent smallest unit of other 6 decimal payment tokens).

Root Cause:

* `pricePerUnit` must be greater than 0
* `pricePerUnit` represents price in payment token’s smallest units for 1 vesting token.
* For USDT (6 decimals), minimum `pricePerUnit` is 1 (0.000001 USDT)
* Prices lower than this cannot be represented

Impact:

1. Cannot list very low-value tokens at their true market price
2. Could prevent legitimate trading of extremely low-value tokens

This limitation will be particularly impactful as memecoins with such low values are fairly common.
