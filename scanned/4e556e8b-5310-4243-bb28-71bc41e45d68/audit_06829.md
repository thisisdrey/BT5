# Replay Attack to Close or Open Market Unexpectedly

## Summary
This bug report is about an issue that was fixed by the team in a recent update. The problem was caused by a lack of security measures in the code, which allowed for potential replay attacks. To fix this, the team made changes to the code that handles the "market pause" functionality, which is used for trading pairs that are not active 24/7. The fix involves incorporating the pause and unpause actions into the generic oracle data flow and implementing a lazy payment funding system. However, this approach may not be effective for low-volume markets, so manual actions may be required to ensure payment funding occurs as expected. The affected files are `vamm.fc`, `vamm/handlers.fc`, and `vamm/oracle-utils.fc`. The bug report recommends implementing a nonce mechanism to prevent replay attacks in the future.

## Details
**Update**
The team fixed the issue in the following commits: `d1c563c`, `7cbb1bc`, `9a0de5c`, `0efabf1`, and `495a4d6`.

To address the issue, the team moved the responsibility for the "market pause" from a specific operation instruction to the pause_at and unpause_at oracle data, incorporating it into the generic oracle data flow. The "market pause" functionality is designed to support trading pairs that do not trade 24/7. After the change, payment funding is now calculated lazily, triggered whenever the condition is met and there is an oracle price data update. This approach seems effective when transaction frequency is high but could present challenges in low-volume markets, where the team may need to manually trigger actions to ensure payment funding occurs with the expected frequency.

Note that the admin can still pause the contract, but these actions do not rely on signatures and thus will not have the replay attack concern.

**File(s) affected:**`vamm.fc`, `vamm/handlers.fc`, `vamm/oracle-utils.fc`

**Description:** In `vamm/handlers.fc`, the `handle::pause()` and `handle::unpause()` functions check that the signature count of `status_msg` exceeds half of the oracles. However, the pause and unpause messages only contain two data fields: `is_active` and `asset_id`. The messages do not include data such as a nonce to prevent replay attacks. As a result, the same signature can be reused to close or open the market via the `op::open_market` or `op::close_market` operations in `vamm.fc`. Moreover, these functions can be called by anyone, making it easy for any attacker to re-open or re-close the market if such an event occurs.

**Recommendation:** Consider applying a nonce mechanism to the pause/unpause messages.
