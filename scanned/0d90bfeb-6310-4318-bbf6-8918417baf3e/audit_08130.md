# Fund Access Restrictions

## Summary
The bug report discusses an issue with the set_funds_recovery_address and authorize_invoke instructions in the Infinex system. These instructions require a signature from the infinex_authority account, which is managed by Infinex. However, if the administrator withholds their signature, users are unable to configure a recovery address and withdraw their funds to a different account. The suggested solution is to revise the constraints on the infinex_authority signature requirements to allow users to withdraw their funds without explicit permission from Infinex. The issue has been resolved in the 8c1b42b patch.

## Details
## Set Funds Recovery Address

The `set_funds_recovery_address` instruction requires a signature from the `infinex_authority` account, an administrator-controlled account managed by Infinex. Similarly, the `authorize_invoke` instruction also requires it to be signed by `infinex_authority`, except in cases where a withdrawal is directed to the user’s designated recovery account. However, if the administrator withholds their signature for both instructions, users would be unable to configure a recovery address and, as a result, would be restricted from withdrawing their funds from their Infinex-managed wallet to any other address.

## Remediation

Revise the constraints on `infinex_authority` signature requirements to make them less strict, allowing users to withdraw their funds to an account of their choice without requiring explicit permission from Infinex.

## Patch

Resolved in `8c1b42b`
