# Incorrect accounting when users of a staking entity stake into that entity

## Summary
This bug report discusses an issue with staking on a platform called LibTokenizedVaultStaking. Due to missing checks, it is possible for users to stake on behalf of an entity, leading to accounting issues. The report recommends adding checks or redesigning the accounting to prevent this issue. The bug has been marked as fixed by the client.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Fixed" by the client. Addressed in: `247aacecd3fd2328753ae26c0bc5ef387cbd12c2`.

**File(s) affected:**`contracts-v3/LibTokenizedVaultStaking.sol`

**Description:** As staking happens on behalf of a users' entity and due to missing checks, it is possible for users of a staking entity to stake into their entity on its behalf. In particular, this could lead to following accounting issues:

1.   `LibTokenizedVaultStaking._stake()`: Double accounting in `L124`, `L129` and `L142-L147`.
2.   `AppStorage.stakeCollected[][]`: Variable is used to track a staker's last collected interval as well as a staking entity's last paid out reward interval, leading to incorrect last interval accounting for users of a staking entity.

**Recommendation:** We recommend adding checks to prevent users of a staking entity from staking into it or consider redesigning the above mentioned accounting to prevent accounting issues, if such a use case is desired.
