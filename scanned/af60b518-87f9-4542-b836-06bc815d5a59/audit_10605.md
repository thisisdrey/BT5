# Users Cannot Claim Rewards From Old Versions

## Summary
The client has acknowledged a bug where users are losing their unclaimed funds after the claiming period has ended. This is due to the fact that the Merkle roots are being overwritten when a new round is created. The recommendation is to store the Merkle roots for each round separately, and to track claims through a mapping system. This will ensure that unclaimed funds are accounted for in future distributions.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Acknowledged" by the client. The client provided the following explanation:

> Acknowledged, users lose their unclaimed funds after the claiming period is over

**File(s) affected:**`ReachDistributionV2.sol`

**Description:** Distributions are held in different rounds, where a user must submit their Merkle proof to claim their rewards for the current round. If the round has elapsed through calling `createDistribution()`, the Merkle roots are overwritten. Though the funds remain in the contract for a future distribution, there is no guarantee that the new Merkle roots will account for unclaimed prizes.

**Recommendation:** Rather than storing one `MerkleTrees` struct for the current active round, consider storing a `mapping(uint256 => MerkleTrees)` that tracks the roots for each version. Claims can be tracked through a `mapping(uint256 => mapping(address => bool))` to track whether an address has claimed a specific version.
